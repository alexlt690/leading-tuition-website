Private Tuition in Kingston upon Thames — Understanding the Local Landscape

Kingston upon Thames sits at the heart of one of the most educationally ambitious corners of South West London. Families here are acutely aware of the academic milestones their children face — from the fiercely competitive 11+ process to GCSE and A-Level examinations that will shape university options. Tiffin School and Tiffin Girls' School are among the most academically competitive grammar schools in England, drawing hundreds of applicants from across South West London and Surrey each year for a very small number of places. Whether you live in Surbiton, New Malden, Norbiton, or further into the borough, the pressure to prepare thoroughly and early is something most local parents understand all too well. At Leading Tuition, we work with families across Kingston upon Thames who want structured, expert support — not generic worksheets, but tuition that genuinely reflects the schools, syllabuses, and expectations your child is navigating.

GCSE and A-Level Support

The majority of secondary schools in and around Kingston upon Thames follow AQA and OCR specifications at both GCSE and A-Level. Whether your child is working through OCR A-Level Chemistry, AQA GCSE English Literature, or OCR Mathematics, our tutors are matched to the specific exam board and qualification level your child is studying — not just the subject in general. This matters more than many parents realise. Mark schemes, assessment objectives, and the style of questions vary significantly between boards, and a tutor who knows the OCR mark scheme inside out will prepare your child very differently from one who teaches generically.

Students at Kingston Grammar School, Surbiton High School, and Holy Cross School often come to us at different stages of their GCSE journey — some needing to consolidate foundations in Year 9, others requiring intensive support in the final months before examinations. We tailor the pace and focus accordingly. At A-Level, where the jump in demand is considerable, our tutors help students develop the analytical depth and independent thinking that top grades require.

11+ and Selective School Entry in Kingston upon Thames

The 11+ in Kingston upon Thames is not a single, uniform test. Tiffin School and Tiffin Girls' School each run their own admissions assessments, and both are known for their rigour. Tiffin School uses a two-stage process: an initial reasoning paper followed by a second stage for those who qualify, assessing English and mathematics in greater depth. Tiffin Girls' School similarly uses a staged assessment. Both schools attract applicants from well beyond the immediate borough, which means your child is competing against a large and well-prepared cohort.

Preparation needs to begin early — typically in Year 4 or the start of Year 5 — and it needs to be targeted. Our tutors work with families in Surbiton, New Malden, and across the borough to build the verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, and core literacy and numeracy skills these assessments demand. Crucially, we also help children develop the stamina and confidence to perform under timed conditions, which is where many able children struggle on the day itself.

Our approach to 11+ preparation includes:

  • A detailed initial assessment to identify your child's current strengths and gaps
  • Structured practice with past and practice papers specific to Tiffin-style assessments
  • Regular progress reviews shared with parents so you always know where your child stands
  • Timed exam practice to build confidence and accuracy under pressure
  • Guidance for parents on what to expect at each stage of the admissions process

University and Medicine Admissions Support

For older students, the transition from A-Level to university application brings its own set of challenges. Students applying to competitive courses — Medicine, Law, Engineering, Economics — often need support that goes beyond their school's standard provision. Our tutors help with UCAT preparation for students applying to medical school, as well as subject-specific admissions tests such as the LNAT for Law and the MAT for Mathematics at Oxford.

We also support personal statement development, helping students articulate their academic interests with clarity and genuine depth. For families in Kingston upon Thames whose children are aiming for Russell Group or Oxbridge entry, this stage of preparation is every bit as important as the A-Level grades themselves.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which GCSE exam boards are most commonly used in Kingston upon Thames schools?

The majority of schools in and around Kingston upon Thames use AQA and OCR at GCSE level. The specific board varies by school and subject, so it is worth checking your child's school directly. Our tutors are matched to the exact specification your child is studying, ensuring that practice and feedback align with the actual mark scheme they will be assessed against.

When should we start preparing for the Tiffin School or Tiffin Girls' School 11+ assessments?

Most families begin structured preparation in Year 4 or early Year 5, which allows around 12 to 18 months of focused work before the assessments take place in Year 6. Starting earlier gives children time to build skills gradually rather than cramming, and it allows space to identify and address any specific weaknesses without undue pressure.

Are sessions available online as well as in person?

Yes. We offer both online and in-person tuition, and many families in Kingston upon Thames use a combination of both depending on the week. Our online sessions use interactive tools that allow tutors and students to work through problems together in real time — they are not simply video calls with a worksheet attached.

How quickly can we expect to see progress?

This depends on the student, the subject, and what they are working towards. Many families notice increased confidence and engagement within the first few sessions. Measurable academic progress — improved test scores, stronger written work, greater independence — typically becomes clear within six to eight weeks of consistent tuition. We share regular feedback so you can track this throughout.
