We don't place tutors based on availability alone. We match them based on the student's specific syllabus, their school's teaching approach, and what they're ultimately working towards.

Medicine, Oxbridge, and University Admissions Coaching

Interest in medicine preparation has grown substantially among Portsmouth families in recent years, and it's easy to understand why. The path to medical school is long, demanding, and highly competitive. Students need strong A-Level grades, a credible personal statement, and a solid performance in the UCAT — the admissions test now used by the majority of UK medical schools, including those at Southampton and Exeter, which many Portsmouth students target.

Leading Tuition offers structured UCAT coaching that goes well beyond practice questions. Our tutors help students understand the reasoning behind each section, develop time management strategies, and build the kind of test-day composure that makes a real difference. We also support students applying to Oxford and Cambridge, working on admissions tests, interview preparation, and the academic depth that Oxbridge tutors expect to see.

For students at Portsmouth Grammar School or Mayfield School with serious university ambitions, this level of preparation is increasingly the norm rather than the exception. We help families approach the process with clarity rather than anxiety.

A-Level and GCSE Tuition in Portsmouth

The majority of Portsmouth secondary schools follow AQA and OCR specifications at both GCSE and A-Level, and our tutors are thoroughly familiar with both. Whether a student is working through OCR A-Level Biology, AQA GCSE Maths, or OCR A-Level Chemistry — subjects that frequently come up among families preparing for competitive university applications — we match them with a tutor who knows that specification in detail, not just the subject in general.

GCSE is often the point at which families first seek support, particularly in Years 10 and 11 when the volume of content increases sharply. A-Level brings a different kind of pressure: the jump in difficulty from GCSE is steeper than many students anticipate, and the stakes are higher. Students at Purbrook Park School and across Portsmouth's sixth forms are working towards university offers that depend on specific grade thresholds. Our tutors help students meet those thresholds with confidence, not last-minute cramming.

Primary, 11+, and Early Secondary Support

Portsmouth does not have selective grammar schools, so families here are not typically preparing for a formal 11+ examination. That said, many parents want their primary-age children to build strong foundations in literacy and numeracy — and to make a confident transition into secondary school. Leading Tuition supports younger students with exactly this in mind: building core skills, developing good study habits, and ensuring that the move into Year 7 feels manageable rather than overwhelming. For students in early secondary who are finding the step up more challenging than expected, early intervention makes a meaningful difference.

Common Questions from Portsmouth Parents

How do you match a tutor to my child?

We take time to understand your child's current school, the specific syllabus and exam board they're following, their academic goals, and how they learn best. We then identify tutors whose subject expertise and teaching style are a strong fit. If the match doesn't feel right after the first session, we'll find an alternative — no pressure, no awkwardness.

My child wants to apply to medical school — can you help with UCAT preparation?

Yes. We offer dedicated UCAT coaching that covers all five sections of the test: Verbal Reasoning, Decision Making, Quantitative Reasoning, Abstract Reasoning, and Situational Judgement. Our tutors help students develop both the skills and the pacing strategies needed to perform well under timed conditions, and we can integrate this with broader medical school application support if needed.

Which A-Level subjects do your tutors cover?

We cover the full range of A-Level subjects commonly taken by Portsmouth students, including Maths, Further Maths, Biology, Chemistry, Physics, English Literature, History, Economics, Psychology, and Geography. If your child is taking a less common subject or a specific exam board variant, get in touch and we'll confirm whether we have the right tutor available.

My child is already doing well — is tuition still worthwhile?

Absolutely. Some of the students who benefit most from working with a specialist tutor are those who are already performing strongly but want to push into the top grade boundaries, strengthen a specific area of weakness within an otherwise solid subject, or prepare seriously for a competitive university application. Tuition isn't only for students who are struggling — it's for students who want to reach their potential.
