Private Tuition in Brighton — Understanding the Local Landscape

Brighton is a city with a genuinely distinctive educational character. Families here navigate a mix of high-performing state schools, well-regarded sixth form colleges, and some of the most academically ambitious independent schools in the South East. At the same time, many Brighton students are setting their sights on competitive universities in London and across the Russell Group — and the pressure to perform at GCSE and A-Level reflects that ambition. Whether your child attends Brighton College, Dorothy Stringer School, or BHASVIC, the academic expectations are real, and the competition for places at top universities is fierce. Leading Tuition works with families across Brighton and the surrounding areas — including Hove, Kemptown, Fiveways, and Preston Park — to provide structured, expert support that makes a measurable difference.

GCSE and A-Level Support

The majority of secondary schools and sixth forms in Brighton and the wider Sussex area follow OCR and Edexcel specifications, and our tutors know these syllabuses in detail. That matters more than it might sound. A tutor who understands the precise command words Edexcel uses in a Biology paper, or how OCR structures its A-Level History coursework, can help your child work more strategically — not just harder.

At GCSE level, we support students across all core and optional subjects, with particular demand for Maths, English, the sciences, and Modern Foreign Languages. Many of the students we work with are aiming for the grades needed to secure a place at a competitive sixth form, including Brighton and Hove High School or Varndean Sixth Form College, where entry expectations are high.

At A-Level, the stakes rise considerably. Students at BHASVIC and Varndean are often targeting universities where grade requirements leave very little margin. Our A-Level tutors are subject specialists — many with first-class degrees or postgraduate qualifications — and they work closely with students to close specific gaps, build exam technique, and develop the kind of independent thinking that top universities reward.

We also support students with:

  • Subject-specific coursework and extended project qualifications
  • Revision planning and exam technique for linear assessment formats
  • Catch-up support after illness, school transitions, or disrupted learning
  • Stretch work for students aiming for A* grades in competitive subjects

11+ and Selective School Entry in Brighton

Brighton and Hove does not have grammar schools, so there is no local 11+ in the traditional sense. However, that does not mean selective entry is irrelevant to Brighton families. Independent schools such as Brighton College use their own entrance assessments, and some families consider schools in neighbouring areas that do operate selective admissions. Leading Tuition supports primary-age children and early secondary students with the foundational skills — reasoning, reading comprehension, written expression, and mathematical fluency — that underpin strong performance in any kind of assessment. We approach this as genuine academic development, not narrow test preparation.

University and Medicine Admissions Support

For students in Brighton applying to highly competitive university courses — including Medicine, Law, Economics, and Engineering — strong A-Level grades are necessary but rarely sufficient. Our university admissions support covers the full picture: personal statement guidance, subject-specific interview preparation, and support with admissions tests where required.

Students applying to study Medicine will need to prepare for the UCAT, which is now used by the majority of UK medical schools, including those favoured by Brighton students targeting London institutions. We work with students on both the cognitive and time-management demands of the UCAT, as well as the structured thinking required in MMI-style interviews. Oxford and Cambridge applicants receive tailored preparation that reflects the specific intellectual rigour those institutions expect at interview.

How Our Tutors Work with Brighton Families

We know that Brighton parents are busy, and that trust matters enormously when you are inviting someone to support your child's education. Every tutor we place has been carefully vetted — academically, professionally, and in terms of how they actually communicate with young people. We do not believe in a one-size-fits-all approach. Before any tuition begins, we take time to understand your child's current position, their specific goals, and how they learn best.

Sessions are available both online and in person across Brighton and Hove. Many families in areas like Lewes and the surrounding villages find online tuition particularly practical, while families closer to the city centre often prefer face-to-face sessions. Both formats are equally effective when the tutor is well-matched and the sessions are properly structured.

We also keep parents informed. You will not be left wondering whether the sessions are working — we provide regular feedback and are always available to discuss progress or adjust the approach if needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which GCSE exam boards are most common in Brighton schools?

Most secondary schools in Brighton and the wider Sussex area use OCR and Edexcel for their GCSE and A-Level courses, though some subjects — particularly sciences — may follow AQA specifications depending on the school. Our tutors are familiar with all major exam boards and will always tailor their support to the specific syllabus your child is studying. If you are unsure which board your child's school uses, we can help you find out.

My child is at primary school — is it too early to think about tuition?

Not at all, though the focus at primary level is quite different. Brighton does not have grammar schools, so there is no local 11+ to prepare for. What we offer for younger students is support with core literacy and numeracy skills, building confidence and strong habits that make secondary school transition much smoother. For families considering independent school entry, we can also help with the specific requirements of those assessments.

Are online sessions as effective as in-person tuition?

For the right student and the right tutor, yes. Online sessions offer flexibility without sacrificing quality — our tutors use interactive tools, shared documents, and structured lesson formats that keep students engaged. That said, some younger students or those who find it harder to focus at home do benefit from in-person sessions, and we are happy to discuss what is likely to work best for your child specifically.

How quickly can I expect to see progress?

It depends on the subject, the student, and what the starting point is — but most families notice a shift in confidence within the first few sessions, even before exam results reflect it. Meaningful grade improvement typically becomes visible over six to ten weeks of consistent work. We are honest with families about realistic timelines, and we would rather set accurate expectations than overpromise.
