11+ and Grammar School Preparation in Barnet

The 11+ in this part of North London is genuinely competitive. Queen Elizabeth's School Barnet and Henrietta Barnett School both use selective admissions processes that test verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, mathematics, and English comprehension. The assessments are designed to be difficult to cram for — but that doesn't mean preparation doesn't matter. It means preparation needs to be intelligent and consistent rather than last-minute and frantic.

We typically recommend starting structured 11+ preparation in Year 4 or early Year 5. This gives enough time to build genuine fluency in the reasoning skills being tested, rather than simply drilling past papers in the final weeks. Our tutors help students develop the underlying skills — logical thinking, reading for inference, mental arithmetic — while also familiarising them with the format and timing of the actual tests. We also work with parents to set realistic expectations and keep the process as calm as possible for the child.

For families considering independent school entry alongside grammar school applications, we also support preparation for schools such as Mill Hill School and Haberdashers' Boys' School, both of which have their own entrance assessments and expect strong academic foundations.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Barnet

How early should we start preparing for the 11+ in Barnet?

We recommend beginning structured preparation in Year 4 or at the very latest the start of Year 5. Starting earlier allows time to build the reasoning and comprehension skills that underpin the tests at QE and Henrietta Barnett, rather than relying on intensive drilling close to the exam date. A steadier, longer preparation is almost always less stressful for the child and tends to produce better results.

Which subjects do Barnet families most often request tuition for?

At primary level, 11+ preparation — covering verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, maths, and English — is by far the most common request. At secondary level, GCSE Maths and English are most frequently requested, followed by the sciences and A-Level Maths. We also see consistent demand for A-Level Chemistry and Biology from students considering medicine or science degrees.

Are your tutors DBS checked?

Yes. Every tutor working with Leading Tuition holds a current enhanced DBS (Disclosure and Barring Service) check. We take safeguarding seriously and this is a non-negotiable part of our vetting process, alongside checking qualifications and conducting our own interviews before any tutor works with a student.

How often should my child have tuition sessions?

For most students, one session per week provides a meaningful level of support without adding pressure to an already busy schedule. During intensive periods — such as the final weeks before 11+ assessments or in the run-up to GCSE or A-level exams — some families choose to increase to two sessions per week. We'll give you an honest recommendation based on your child's specific situation rather than a one-size-fits-all answer.
