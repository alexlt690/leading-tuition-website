Private Tuition in Leicester — Understanding the Local Landscape

Leicester is a city with real academic ambition. Families across Oadby, Stoneygate, and Knighton are navigating a competitive educational landscape — from preparing children for entry into independent schools, to supporting GCSE and A-Level students aiming for Russell Group universities and, in particular, the University of Leicester's medical school. That pressure is felt early and builds steadily. At Leading Tuition, we work with Leicester families at every stage of that journey, offering structured, expert support that is tailored to the specific schools, exam boards, and academic milestones that matter most in this city.

GCSE and A-Level Support

The majority of secondary schools in Leicester and the surrounding area follow AQA and OCR specifications, and our tutors are thoroughly familiar with both. Whether your child is sitting AQA GCSE English Language, OCR A-Level Chemistry, or working through AQA Mathematics at either tier, our tutors plan sessions around the exact specification your child is being assessed on — not a generic version of the subject.

Students at schools such as Gartree School in Oadby often come to us during Year 10 or Year 11 when the volume of content starts to feel unmanageable. Others join us earlier, in Year 9, to build the kind of secure foundations that make the GCSE years far less stressful. At A-Level, the stakes are higher and the jump in difficulty is real. Our tutors help students develop the analytical depth and independent thinking that top grades — and competitive university applications — genuinely require.

We support students across a wide range of GCSE and A-Level subjects, including:

  • Mathematics and Further Mathematics
  • Biology, Chemistry, and Physics
  • English Language and English Literature
  • History, Geography, and Economics
  • Modern Foreign Languages, including French and Spanish

If your child's subject is not listed here, please do get in touch — our tutor network is broad, and we can usually find the right match.

11+ and Selective School Entry in Leicester

Leicester itself does not have grammar schools, so the 11+ in its traditional form is not a feature of the local state system. However, many families in areas such as Stoneygate and Oadby are preparing their children for entry into independent schools, where admissions assessments and interviews form a significant part of the process.

Leicester Grammar School, based in Great Glen, is one of the most academically selective independent schools in the region. Entry at 11 is competitive, and children benefit from structured preparation in verbal and non-verbal reasoning, mathematics, and English comprehension. Similarly, families considering Stoneygate School or planning ahead for senior independent school entry — including Loughborough Grammar School — often begin working with a tutor well before the assessment date.

Leading Tuition supports primary-age children and early secondary students with the kind of careful, confidence-building preparation that makes a real difference in these assessments. We focus not just on content, but on helping children approach unfamiliar question types calmly and methodically — a skill that serves them well beyond any single exam.

University and Medicine Admissions Support

Leicester has a strong tradition of producing students who go on to study medicine, and the University of Leicester Medical School remains one of the most sought-after destinations for aspiring doctors in the East Midlands. Getting there requires more than strong A-Level grades. Students need to perform well in the UCAT (University Clinical Aptitude Test), write a compelling personal statement, and handle medical school interviews with genuine composure.

Our tutors include graduates and professionals with direct experience of the medical admissions process. We offer focused UCAT preparation, personal statement guidance, and mock interview practice — all tailored to the specific demands of medical school entry. For students targeting other Russell Group universities, we also support UCAS applications more broadly, helping students articulate their academic interests clearly and persuasively.

For those considering Oxford or Cambridge, we are fully up to date with current admissions requirements. Both universities now use the UCAT for medicine, and we prepare students accordingly — alongside subject-specific admissions tests and interview preparation for other courses.

How Our Tutors Work with Leicester Families

We know that families in Wigston, Evington, and across Leicester lead busy lives. Fitting tuition around school, extracurricular commitments, and family schedules is a practical concern, not a minor one. That is why we offer both online and in-person sessions, giving families the flexibility to choose what works best for them without compromising on quality.

Every student who joins Leading Tuition is matched with a tutor based on their subject needs, learning style, and personality — not simply on availability. Before sessions begin, we take time to understand where your child is now, what they are working towards, and what has or has not worked for them in the past. Progress is monitored throughout, and we keep parents informed so that you are never left wondering whether the sessions are making a difference.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which exam boards do Leicester schools typically use, and will my child's tutor know their specific course?

Most secondary schools in Leicester follow AQA or OCR specifications at both GCSE and A-Level. Our tutors work from the exact specification your child is studying, so sessions are directly relevant to their assessments rather than based on a general overview of the subject. When you get in touch, simply let us know your child's school and subjects, and we will confirm the right fit from the outset.

My child is at primary school — is it too early to start thinking about independent school entry?

Not at all. For schools like Leicester Grammar School, where entry at 11 is genuinely competitive, starting preparation in Year 4 or Year 5 gives children time to build skills steadily rather than cramming in the months before assessment. Early support also helps children develop confidence with problem-solving and reasoning, which benefits them academically regardless of the outcome.

Do you offer in-person tuition in Leicester, or is everything online?

We offer both. Many of our Leicester families choose online sessions for the convenience and consistency they provide, particularly when schedules are unpredictable. Others prefer face-to-face sessions, especially for younger children or for subjects where working through problems together on paper feels more natural. We are happy to discuss what would suit your child best.

How quickly can I expect to see progress?

This depends on the student, the subject, and how far away their assessments are — but most families notice a meaningful shift in confidence and engagement within four to six weeks of regular sessions. Academic results tend to follow once that foundation is in place. We set clear, realistic goals from the beginning so that progress feels tangible rather than vague.
