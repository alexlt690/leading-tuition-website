Private Tuition in Cambridge

Expert tutors supporting families across Cambridge

Book a Free Consultation

Cambridge is a city where academic ambition runs deep. Families across Newnham, Trumpington, and beyond are raising children in an environment shaped by one of the world's great universities, and that proximity to excellence creates real pressure at every stage of a child's education. Whether a student is finding their feet in Year 7, preparing for GCSEs at The Perse School or The Stephen Perse Foundation, or working towards a Cambridge University application from Hills Road Sixth Form College, the expectations are high and the competition is serious. Leading Tuition works with Cambridge families across all of these stages — providing structured, subject-specific support that moves with a student as their needs change.

Supporting Cambridge Students from 11+ to University

A student's academic journey in Cambridge rarely follows a simple path. The challenges at primary level are quite different from those at GCSE, and the leap from GCSE to A-Level catches many capable students off guard. What stays constant is the need for clear, expert guidance from someone who understands not just the subject, but the specific pressures a Cambridge student faces.

Leading Tuition supports students from upper primary through to university application. For younger students, that might mean building strong foundations in English and maths before secondary school begins. For older students, it means targeted work on exam technique, subject content, and — for those aiming at the most competitive universities — the kind of deep subject knowledge that admissions tutors are looking for.

Preparing for Selective Schools and the 11+ in Cambridge

Unlike parts of the country where grammar school entrance dominates Year 6, Cambridge does not have a grammar school system requiring 11+ preparation in the traditional sense. However, several of the city's most respected independent schools — including The Perse School and St Mary's School Cambridge — have their own admissions processes, and competition for places is real.

For primary-age students and those entering early secondary, Leading Tuition focuses on building the core academic skills that make a genuine difference: reading comprehension, mathematical reasoning, written expression, and the confidence to perform under pressure. Getting these foundations right early pays dividends throughout a student's school career, regardless of which school they attend.

Raising Grades at GCSE — Cambridge Schools and Exam Boards

GCSE is where many students first encounter the gap between understanding something in class and being able to demonstrate that understanding under exam conditions. In Cambridgeshire, OCR is the dominant exam board across many subjects, and Leading Tuition's tutors are thoroughly familiar with OCR specifications, mark schemes, and the particular demands of OCR assessments in subjects like English, science, and history.

That said, students at different schools across Cambridge may also be sitting AQA or Edexcel papers, and Leading Tuition works across all major boards. Whether a student is at Long Road Sixth Form College's feeder schools, attending The Stephen Perse Foundation, or studying at a local state secondary in Cherry Hinton or Chesterton, the approach is the same: identify exactly where marks are being lost, address the underlying gaps, and build the exam technique to convert knowledge into results.

Common areas where Cambridge students seek GCSE support include:

  • OCR GCSE Sciences — particularly the required practicals and extended response questions
  • GCSE Mathematics, including higher-tier problem solving and algebra
  • English Language and Literature, where analytical writing technique is often the deciding factor
  • Modern Foreign Languages, especially speaking and writing components
  • Humanities subjects such as History and Geography, where essay structure matters as much as content

A-Level Tuition and Sixth Form Support in Cambridge

Hills Road Sixth Form College and Long Road Sixth Form College are two of the strongest sixth forms in the country, and students who earn places there are already capable. But capable students still face real challenges at A-Level — the step up in demand from GCSE is steep, the volume of independent study is greater, and the margin for error in university applications is narrow.

Leading Tuition works with A-Level students across all major subjects, with particular depth in mathematics, the sciences, economics, English, and the humanities. Tutors are matched not just by subject but by exam board — OCR A-Level specifications differ meaningfully from AQA or Edexcel, and that specificity matters when a student is preparing for their actual papers. For students at Hills Road or Long Road, or those studying independently in areas like Newnham or Ely, tuition can be delivered online or in person depending on what works best.

Medical School and Oxbridge Preparation for Cambridge Applicants

Cambridge has an unusually high proportion of students who are genuinely competitive for Oxford, Cambridge, and other leading universities. This is not wishful thinking — it reflects the quality of schools like Hills Road and The Perse School, and the culture of academic seriousness that runs through the city. But aiming for Oxbridge or a top medical school requires preparation that goes well beyond strong A-Level grades.

Leading Tuition provides dedicated support for students navigating this process. That includes admissions test preparation — the MAT for Mathematics at Oxford, the LNAT for Law, the TSA for courses at Oxford and Cambridge, the HAT for History at Oxford, and the ELAT for English at Oxford. It also includes interview preparation, which is a distinct skill: the ability to think aloud, engage with unfamiliar problems, and demonstrate genuine intellectual curiosity under pressure.

For students applying to medicine, Leading Tuition supports UCAT preparation alongside the broader personal statement and interview process. The goal is not to coach students into sounding a certain way, but to help them articulate what they already know and think — clearly, confidently, and in the way that admissions tutors are actually looking for.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Leading Tuition cover my child's specific school and exam board?

Yes. Leading Tuition works with students across Cambridge's state and independent schools, including The Perse School, Hills Road Sixth Form College, Long Road Sixth Form College, St Mary's School Cambridge, and The Stephen Perse Foundation, as well as local state secondaries. Tutors are matched by subject and exam board — whether your child is sitting OCR, AQA, or Edexcel, their tutor will know the specification in detail.

My child is moving from GCSE to A-Level — when should we start tuition?

The transition from GCSE to A-Level is one of the most common points where students benefit from early support. Starting at the beginning of Year 12, rather than waiting until difficulties emerge, allows a tutor to help a student build the right study habits and subject depth from the outset. Many students who struggled at GCSE find that structured A-Level tuition from the start makes a significant difference to their confidence and results.

Is it possible to arrange intensive revision support close to exams?

Yes. Leading Tuition can arrange focused revision programmes in the weeks before exams, working through past papers, mark schemes, and the specific topics most likely to appear. Intensive support works best when it is targeted — a tutor will quickly identify where a student's time is best spent rather than covering ground they already know well.

What happens if my child's grades do not improve?

Leading Tuition takes this seriously. If progress is slower than expected, the first step is an honest conversation about what is and is not working — whether that is the approach, the frequency of sessions, or factors outside tuition that are affecting a student's performance. Tutors are expected to adapt, and parents are kept informed throughout. The aim is always to find what genuinely helps a particular student, not to continue with an approach that is not delivering results.
