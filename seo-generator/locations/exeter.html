Private Tuition in Exeter — Understanding the Local Landscape

Exeter is a city with genuine academic ambition. The University of Exeter — one of the most sought-after Russell Group institutions in the South West — sits at the heart of that ambition, and its presence shapes the expectations of local families from secondary school onwards. Parents across the city, from Heavitree to Topsham, are acutely aware that strong A-Level results open doors not just to Exeter's own campus, but to universities across the country. At the same time, competitive entry into independent schools such as Exeter School and The Maynard School means that for many families, the pressure begins well before sixth form. Leading Tuition works with families across Exeter who are navigating exactly these pressures — and we understand that the stakes feel very real.

GCSE and A-Level Support

The majority of secondary schools in Exeter — including St Luke's Science and Sports College and Clyst Vale Community College — follow OCR specifications at GCSE and A-Level. This matters more than many parents realise. OCR mark schemes, command words, and assessment structures have their own logic, and a tutor who knows the specification inside out will prepare your child far more effectively than one working from generic notes.

Our tutors are matched to the specific OCR courses your child is studying — whether that is OCR GCSE Combined Science, OCR A-Level Mathematics, or OCR English Language. For students at Exeter College, where A-Level cohorts are large and class sizes can make individual support harder to access, one-to-one tuition gives students the focused attention that makes a genuine difference to their grades and their confidence.

We also support students following AQA and Edexcel courses where schools have chosen those boards, so if your child's school uses a different specification, we will match them with a tutor who knows it thoroughly.

Common areas where Exeter students seek support include:

  • OCR A-Level Biology and Chemistry, particularly for students with aspirations in medicine or healthcare
  • GCSE Mathematics, where the jump from Key Stage 3 to higher-tier GCSE content catches many students off guard
  • A-Level English Literature and History, where essay technique and independent argument are assessed heavily
  • GCSE and A-Level French and Spanish, especially for students aiming for strong language grades alongside sciences

11+ and Selective School Entry in Exeter

Unlike some parts of the country, Exeter does not have a grammar school system requiring the 11+ as a standard hurdle. However, that does not mean selective entry is irrelevant to local families. Entry into Exeter School and The Maynard School at 11 or 13 involves competitive assessments, and preparation for these requires structured, subject-specific support rather than generic 11+ drilling.

Leading Tuition works with primary-age children and early secondary students to build the core skills — reasoning, reading comprehension, mathematical fluency, and written expression — that underpin both independent school assessments and a strong start to secondary education. If your child is in Years 4 to 7 and you want to give them a confident foundation, we can put together a programme that fits their current level and your timeline.

University and Medicine Admissions Support

For sixth-form students in Exeter with ambitions towards competitive university courses, the admissions process extends well beyond predicted grades. Students applying to study Medicine, Dentistry, or Veterinary Science will need to prepare for the UCAT — the admissions test now used by the majority of UK medical schools, including those at Oxford, Cambridge, and Imperial. UCAT preparation requires consistent, timed practice across five distinct subtests, and many students significantly underestimate how much structured preparation it demands.

Beyond medicine, students applying to Oxford or Cambridge will face written assessments and interviews that reward deep subject knowledge and the ability to think under pressure. Our tutors include graduates of these universities who understand what admissions tutors are genuinely looking for — and can help your child develop that level of intellectual confidence before interview day.

We also support personal statement drafting, helping students from across Exeter articulate their academic interests in a way that is specific, honest, and compelling.

How Our Tutors Work with Exeter Families

We know that families in areas like Pinhoe and Exwick are often juggling school runs, work commitments, and the logistics of getting children to and from activities. That is why we offer both online and in-person tuition, and why we work hard to match your child with a tutor whose availability fits your family's routine rather than the other way around.

Every student who joins Leading Tuition begins with a careful assessment of where they are now — not just academically, but in terms of how they learn, what is making them anxious, and what they need to feel more in control of their studies. From there, we build a programme with clear goals and regular progress updates, so you always know how things are moving forward.

Our tutors are subject specialists, not generalists. A student preparing for OCR A-Level Chemistry will work with someone who knows that specification in depth — not a tutor who covers every subject and none particularly well.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which GCSE exam boards do Exeter schools typically use?

The dominant exam board across Exeter secondary schools is OCR, and our tutors are experienced with OCR specifications at both GCSE and A-Level. Some schools use AQA or Edexcel for particular subjects, and we match students with tutors who know their specific board and course — not just the subject in general.

Does my child need 11+ preparation if we are not applying to a grammar school?

Exeter does not have grammar schools, so the 11+ is not a standard requirement here. However, if you are considering entry to Exeter School or The Maynard School, those schools run their own competitive assessments. We can support your child with targeted preparation for independent school entry, as well as broader primary and early secondary support to build strong academic foundations.

Do your tutors offer online sessions, or is tuition in person only?

We offer both. Many Exeter families find online sessions more practical — particularly for students with busy after-school schedules or for families in areas where travel adds up. Online tuition with Leading Tuition uses an interactive whiteboard and is every bit as structured and effective as in-person work. Where in-person sessions are preferred and logistically straightforward, we can discuss that too.

How quickly will my child start to see progress?

This depends on the student, the subject, and how far away key assessments are — but most families notice a shift in confidence within the first three or four sessions. Measurable grade improvement typically follows over six to ten weeks of consistent work. We set clear short-term goals from the outset so that progress feels tangible rather than vague, and we keep you informed at every stage.
