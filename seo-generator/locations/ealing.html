Ealing sits at the heart of West London's educational ambitions. Families across the borough — from Acton and Northfields to Hanwell and Greenford — are navigating a school landscape that is both richly varied and genuinely competitive. Whether a child is working towards GCSEs at Drayton Manor High School or Greenford High School, preparing for A-Levels at a sixth form, or aiming for a place at one of the borough's highly regarded independent schools such as St Benedict's or Notting Hill and Ealing High School, the academic expectations are high and the pressure on students is real. Many Ealing families are also thinking ahead to university — including London's leading institutions — and want their children to build the strongest possible foundation from an early age. At Leading Tuition, we understand this landscape in detail, and we work with families across the borough to provide structured, subject-specific support that makes a measurable difference.

Private Tuition in Ealing — Understanding the Local Landscape

Ealing is a borough of high aspirations. It is home to a mix of strong state comprehensives, respected independent schools, and a growing number of families who take their children's academic progress seriously. Schools like Drayton Manor High School and Greenford High School consistently produce strong GCSE results, and the competition for places at sixth forms and universities means that students cannot afford to fall behind at any stage. At the same time, independent schools such as St Benedict's School and Notting Hill and Ealing High School attract families from across West London, and entry to these schools requires careful, sustained preparation.

The dominant exam boards in Ealing's state schools are AQA and Edexcel, and our tutors are thoroughly familiar with both — including the specific mark schemes, question styles, and assessment objectives that examiners use. This matters because generic subject knowledge is not enough. A student sitting an Edexcel GCSE Mathematics paper needs to practise the right kinds of questions in the right format. Our tutors know the difference, and they plan sessions accordingly.

GCSE and A-Level Support

GCSE and A-Level years are the stages where structured tuition tends to have the greatest impact. At GCSE, students are often managing eight or nine subjects simultaneously, and it is common for one or two areas to fall behind while others progress well. Our tutors work with students to identify exactly where the gaps are — whether that is algebra and ratio in Maths, source analysis in History, or essay structure in English Literature — and then build a focused plan to address them.

At A-Level, the step up in difficulty catches many students off guard, even those who performed well at GCSE. The volume of content, the expectation of independent thinking, and the pressure of university applications all arrive at once. Our A-Level tutors are subject specialists who understand the demands of AQA and Edexcel qualifications at this level, and who can help students develop both the knowledge and the exam technique they need to perform confidently.

  • GCSE support across core and optional subjects, including Maths, Sciences, English, History, and Modern Languages
  • A-Level tuition in a wide range of subjects, tailored to AQA and Edexcel specifications
  • Targeted help with past papers, mark schemes, and examiner feedback
  • Support for students who are resitting or catching up after illness or disruption

11+ and Selective School Entry in Ealing

Ealing does not have grammar schools, so the 11+ examination is not a feature of the local state system in the way it is in neighbouring areas. However, a number of families in the borough do pursue selective independent school entry, and preparation for schools such as St Benedict's or Notting Hill and Ealing High School requires genuine academic readiness rather than last-minute cramming. Leading Tuition supports primary-age children and early secondary students with the kind of structured, progressive learning that builds real ability — in reasoning, reading comprehension, and mathematical thinking — rather than simply drilling test formats. We also work with Year 7 and Year 8 students who are settling into secondary school and want to establish strong habits early.

University and Medicine Admissions Support

For students in Ealing with ambitions to study at competitive universities — including UCL, Imperial, King's College London, and the universities of Oxford and Cambridge — the admissions process extends well beyond A-Level grades. Personal statements, admissions tests, and interviews all play a significant role, and preparation for these requires specialist knowledge.

Students applying for Medicine, Dentistry, or other health-related courses will need to sit the UCAT, which is now used by the majority of UK medical schools including those at Oxford, Cambridge, and Imperial. Our tutors have direct experience supporting students through UCAT preparation, as well as through the personal statement process and medical school interviews. We also support applicants to law, engineering, economics, and other competitive courses where admissions tests or strong written applications are required.

How Our Tutors Work with Ealing Families

We know that parents in Ealing are busy, and that finding the right tutor — and then managing sessions around school, activities, and family life — takes real effort. Our approach is designed to make this as straightforward as possible. Sessions can take place online or in person, depending on what works best for your family. We match students with tutors based on subject expertise, teaching style, and the specific goals of the student, not simply on availability.

From the first session, our tutors focus on understanding where a student currently is, what is holding them back, and what a realistic path forward looks like. Progress is communicated clearly to parents, and we adjust our approach as the student develops. Our aim is always for the student to become more confident and more independent — not more dependent on tuition.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which GCSE exam boards do Ealing schools typically use?

The majority of state schools in Ealing use AQA and Edexcel across their GCSE subjects, though some schools use OCR for certain qualifications. Our tutors are familiar with all three boards and will always tailor their support to the specific specification your child is studying, including the correct question formats and assessment criteria.

Does Leading Tuition help with selective school entry if there are no grammar schools in Ealing?

Yes. While Ealing does not have grammar schools, some families pursue entry to selective independent schools locally or in neighbouring boroughs. We support primary and early secondary students with the underlying academic skills — reasoning, comprehension, and mathematical thinking — that make a genuine difference to selective entry outcomes, rather than focusing narrowly on test formats.

Are sessions available online, or do tutors come to our home?

We offer both. Many Ealing families find online sessions more convenient, particularly for fitting tuition around busy school and activity schedules. Others prefer in-person sessions for younger students or for subjects where working through problems on paper is important. We are happy to discuss what will work best for your child and your household.

How quickly can we expect to see progress?

This depends on the student, the subject, and how far from an exam they are. In our experience, most students begin to feel more confident within three to four sessions, once a tutor has identified the specific gaps and started working through them systematically. Measurable improvement in test scores and class performance typically follows over the course of several weeks of consistent work. We will always give you an honest picture of where your child stands and what is achievable in the time available.
