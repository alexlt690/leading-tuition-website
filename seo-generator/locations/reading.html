Reading sits at the heart of one of England's most educationally ambitious regions. Families across Caversham, Earley, Tilehurst, and Woodley are navigating some of the most competitive academic pathways in the country — from the fiercely contested 11+ entry to Reading School and Kendrick School, two of England's most selective grammar schools, to strong demand for expert GCSE and A-Level support from students preparing for university. Whether your child is sitting AQA or OCR papers, aiming for a grammar school place, or working towards medicine at a Russell Group university, the academic stakes in this part of Berkshire are genuinely high. Leading Tuition works with families across Reading who want more than a tutor — they want a clear plan, consistent progress, and someone who understands exactly what their child is up against.

Private Tuition in Reading — Understanding the Local Landscape

Reading's educational landscape is shaped by a mix of outstanding state schools, selective grammars, and strong independent options. The presence of Reading School and Kendrick School creates an 11+ culture that begins early — many families start preparing in Year 4 or even Year 3. At the same time, schools like The Abbey School Reading and The Oratory School attract families seeking rigorous independent education, where entrance assessments and scholarship exams demand their own focused preparation.

Across the state sector, the majority of students sit AQA and OCR qualifications at GCSE and A-Level. These are demanding syllabuses, and the gap between a grade 5 and a grade 7 often comes down to exam technique and subject-specific confidence as much as raw ability. Our tutors know these specifications in detail — not in a general sense, but the precise mark schemes, common question types, and the areas where students in Reading most often lose marks.

What makes Reading distinctive is the combination of pressures families face simultaneously. A parent in Woodley might have one child preparing for the Kendrick entrance exam while another is sitting A-Level Chemistry at a local sixth form. We work with both, and we understand that each situation calls for a different approach.

GCSE and A-Level Support

At GCSE level, the subjects we most commonly support in Reading include Maths, English Language and Literature, the sciences, History, and Modern Foreign Languages — all predominantly examined through AQA or OCR. Our tutors work directly from the relevant specification, identifying gaps early and building the kind of structured revision that produces consistent results rather than last-minute cramming.

A-Level support is equally important for Reading families, particularly those with children at sixth forms across Berkshire who are targeting competitive university courses. Whether your child is studying OCR A-Level Biology, AQA A-Level Maths, or working through a humanities subject, our tutors bring both subject expertise and an understanding of what top universities expect to see in personal statements and predicted grades.

  • AQA and OCR GCSE Maths, English, and Sciences
  • A-Level support across core and humanities subjects
  • Targeted exam technique and mark scheme training
  • Support for students at The Abbey School Reading and other independent schools with different internal assessment structures
  • Catch-up tuition for students who have fallen behind following illness or disruption

11+ and Selective School Entry in Reading

The 11+ in Reading is not a single, uniform test — and that matters. Reading School and Kendrick School each have their own admissions processes, and the level of preparation required is substantial. Both schools are among the most oversubscribed grammar schools in England, drawing applicants from across Berkshire and beyond. A child sitting the entrance assessment is competing against a large pool of well-prepared candidates, many of whom have been working with tutors for a year or more.

Leading Tuition begins 11+ preparation with a thorough assessment of where your child currently stands across verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, mathematics, and English comprehension. From there, we build a structured programme that develops genuine ability rather than simply drilling past papers. Past papers have their place — but a child who understands the underlying skills will perform far more reliably under exam conditions than one who has only memorised question formats.

We also support families preparing for independent school entrance assessments, including those applying to St Joseph's College Reading, where the admissions process rewards strong core academic skills alongside confidence and independent thinking. Preparation timelines vary, but for grammar school entry, we typically recommend beginning no later than the start of Year 5.

University and Medicine Admissions Support

For older students, Reading's proximity to London and strong local sixth form provision means many families are navigating highly competitive university applications. We support students applying to medicine, dentistry, and other competitive courses with UCAT preparation — the admissions test now used by Oxford, Cambridge, Imperial, and the majority of UK medical schools. Our tutors understand the structure of the UCAT, the timing pressures it creates, and how to build the cognitive skills it tests over a sustained preparation period.

Beyond admissions tests, we work with students on personal statement content, subject-specific interview preparation, and the academic strengthening that makes an application credible at the most selective institutions.

We offer both online and in-person tuition across Reading and the surrounding areas, including Henley-on-Thames and Caversham. Frequently Asked Questions

Which GCSE exam boards are most common in Reading schools?

The majority of state secondary schools in Reading use AQA and OCR across core subjects including Maths, English, and the Sciences. Some schools use Edexcel for certain subjects, particularly in Maths. Our tutors are familiar with all three specifications and will always work from your child's specific syllabus and mark scheme rather than a generic curriculum.

When should we start preparing for the 11+ for Reading School or Kendrick School?

Most families begin structured preparation in Year 4, with some starting earlier if a child needs more time to build confidence in reasoning skills. Both Reading School and Kendrick School are highly selective, and the competition is intense. Starting in Year 5 is still workable, but it requires a focused and consistent programme. We always begin with an honest assessment so you have a realistic picture from the outset.

Do you offer online tuition as well as in-person sessions in Reading?

Yes. We offer both, and many Reading families use a combination depending on the time of year and their child's schedule. Online sessions are conducted via an interactive whiteboard platform and are equally effective for most subjects and age groups. In-person sessions are available across Reading and nearby areas including Earley, Caversham, and Henley-on-Thames, subject to tutor availability.

How quickly can I expect to see progress with a Leading Tuition tutor?

This depends on the subject, the student's starting point, and how frequently sessions take place. For most students, a meaningful shift in confidence and understanding is visible within four to six weeks of consistent weekly tuition. Measurable grade improvement typically follows over a longer period, particularly at GCSE and A-Level where assessments are less frequent. We set clear short-term targets so progress feels tangible well before any formal exam results arrive.
