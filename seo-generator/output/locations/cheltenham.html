GCSE and A-Level Support

Students at Pate's Grammar School are working within one of the most academically demanding state school environments in the country. The pace is exceptional — Pate's consistently achieves some of the highest A-Level results of any state school in England — and students who are finding specific subjects difficult benefit significantly from targeted one-to-one support.

At Cheltenham Ladies' College and Cheltenham College, the internal expectations are similarly high and the sixth-form programmes are genuinely demanding. Gloucestershire schools follow predominantly OCR specifications, and our tutors are matched to students based on their exact board and course. At GCSE and A-Level, we support students across all subjects, with particularly strong tutors in mathematics, further mathematics, sciences, economics, and English.

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

Pate's Grammar School is one of the most academically selective state schools in England, and competition for its limited places is intense. Children apply from across Gloucestershire and beyond, and those who succeed have typically had structured preparation that goes well beyond working through practice papers. The 11+ assessment for Pate's tests verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, English, and Mathematics — and the standard required reflects the school's position at the very top of state school academic tables.

We begin 11+ preparation with an honest assessment of where your child currently stands, then build a structured programme that develops the reasoning skills, mathematical confidence, and English comprehension that the assessment targets. We typically begin formal preparation in Year 4 or early Year 5, giving enough time for genuine skill development rather than surface-level drilling.

For families considering Cheltenham Ladies' College or Cheltenham College, both schools set their own entrance assessments with a different character from the local authority grammar test. We can advise on how to prepare for both routes simultaneously if that is relevant to your situation.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

Pate's, Cheltenham Ladies' College, and Cheltenham College all send significant numbers of students to Oxbridge and competitive degree programmes each year. Leading Tuition supports these students with Oxbridge admissions test coaching (MAT, LNAT, TSA, and others), personal statement development, interview preparation, and UCAT preparation for medicine applications. For students at these schools, admissions preparation is an extension of the academic excellence they are already demonstrating — and our tutors understand that context.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Cheltenham

How competitive is the 11+ for Pate's Grammar School?

Exceptionally competitive. Pate's is consistently ranked in the top five state schools in England, and competition for its places reflects this. The school draws applicants from across Gloucestershire and beyond. Children who gain places have typically had sustained, structured preparation beginning in Year 4 — not a last-minute push — and approach the assessment with well-developed reasoning and examination skills.

My daughter is at Pate's and working towards an Oxbridge application. Can you help?

Yes. Oxbridge preparation — admissions test coaching, personal statement development, and interview preparation — is one of our specialist areas. Students at Pate's are academically very strong, and the preparation that makes the difference at Oxbridge is specific: understanding what the admissions tutors are looking for, developing the ability to reason under pressure in an interview setting, and writing a personal statement that is genuinely compelling rather than conventionally well-crafted.

We are in Charlton Kings — do you cover our area?

Yes. We work with families across Cheltenham, Leckhampton, Charlton Kings, Prestbury, Bishops Cleeve, and the wider Gloucestershire area including Cirencester. Most sessions are conducted online, but in-person is available where preferred.

Can you support a student at Cheltenham College applying to medicine?

Yes. UCAT preparation, personal statement writing, MMI interview coaching, and A-Level support in the sciences are all areas we cover. Cheltenham College has a strong sixth form, and its medicine applicants are competitive — but the UCAT and interview components require specific preparation that is different from A-Level academic skills, and this is where specialist support adds the most value.
