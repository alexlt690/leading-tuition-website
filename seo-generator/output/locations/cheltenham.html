11+ and Grammar School Preparation in Cheltenham

Securing a place at Pate's Grammar School is one of the most sought-after outcomes for primary-age children in Gloucestershire. The school is oversubscribed every year, and the 11+ selection process is genuinely demanding. The assessment tests verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, mathematics, and English — and while raw ability matters, familiarity with the format and question types makes a significant difference to how a child performs on the day.

Leading Tuition works with families from Year 4 onwards to build the skills and confidence needed for 11+ success. Our approach isn't about drilling children through endless practice papers. We focus on understanding where each child's gaps are, strengthening their reasoning skills systematically, and making sure they feel calm and prepared — not anxious — when the assessment arrives. We also help parents understand the timeline, the registration process, and what realistic preparation looks like for their child specifically.

Key areas we cover in 11+ preparation include:

  • Verbal and non-verbal reasoning techniques
  • Mathematical problem-solving at the required level
  • Reading comprehension and written English
  • Timed practice under realistic exam conditions
  • Confidence-building and managing assessment-day nerves

Leading Tuition supports students through every stage of this process. Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Cheltenham

How early should we start preparing for the 11+ in Cheltenham?

Most families begin structured 11+ preparation around the start of Year 5, which gives roughly 12 to 18 months before the assessment. That said, starting in Year 4 is not too early if your child is keen and you want a more gradual, lower-pressure approach. The key is building skills steadily rather than cramming in the final weeks.

Which subjects are most in demand for tutoring in Cheltenham?

At primary level, the focus is almost entirely on 11+ preparation. At GCSE, Mathematics, English, and the Sciences are consistently our most requested subjects. At A-level, we see high demand for Mathematics, Chemistry, and Biology — particularly from students applying to competitive university courses.

Are your tutors DBS checked?

Yes. Every tutor working with Leading Tuition holds a current DBS check, and we verify qualifications and references before any tutor joins our network. Your child's safety and your peace of mind are not optional extras — they are the baseline from which everything else follows.

How often should my child have tuition sessions?

For most students, one session per week provides a meaningful boost without adding unsustainable pressure to an already busy school schedule. In the run-up to important assessments — whether that's the 11+, GCSE mocks, or A-level exams — some families choose to increase to two sessions per week for a defined period. We'll always give you an honest recommendation based on your child's situation rather than a blanket answer.
