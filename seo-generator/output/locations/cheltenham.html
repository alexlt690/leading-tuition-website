Cheltenham is one of the most educationally ambitious towns in England. Families here navigate a school landscape that combines fierce competition for state grammar places — Pate's Grammar School is among the most academically selective state schools in the entire country, drawing applicants from right across Gloucestershire — with one of the most prestigious independent school sectors anywhere outside London, including Cheltenham Ladies' College and Cheltenham College. Whether your child is sitting the 11+, working towards GCSEs, or preparing university applications, the academic expectations in this town are high. Leading Tuition works with families across Cheltenham and the surrounding area to make sure children are genuinely prepared, not just hopeful.

Tutoring in Cheltenham — What We Offer

Leading Tuition provides one-to-one private tuition for pupils from primary age through to sixth form and beyond. Our tutors are subject specialists, not generalists, and every programme is built around your child's specific needs — their school, their syllabus, their timeline, and the gaps that are actually holding them back.

We work with families in Cheltenham itself and in the surrounding communities, including Leckhampton, Charlton Kings, Prestbury, and Bishops Cleeve. Sessions are available online or in person, giving you flexibility without compromising on quality. Many of our Cheltenham families combine both formats — online during busy school weeks, in person when deeper exam preparation is needed.

Our tutors are familiar with the schools and syllabuses your child is actually working with. That local knowledge matters. A tutor who understands the difference between what Cheltenham Bournside School expects at GCSE and what Dean Close School demands at the same level is far better placed to help your child than one working from a generic revision guide.

GCSE and A-Level Support

The majority of state secondary schools in Cheltenham — including Cheltenham Bournside School — follow OCR specifications at GCSE and A-Level. OCR is the dominant exam board in this area, and our tutors know these specifications in detail. That means your child is not just learning the content; they are learning how OCR assesses it, what the mark schemes reward, and how to structure answers that actually score well.

At GCSE, we support the full range of core and option subjects. At A-Level, our most requested subjects in Cheltenham include Mathematics, the Sciences, English Literature, and History — though we cover a wide range beyond these. For pupils at independent schools such as Cheltenham College or Cheltenham Ladies' College, we are equally comfortable with the specific demands of those institutions, which often set their own internal assessments alongside public examinations.

We also support pupils studying the International Baccalaureate, which is offered at several independent schools in the area. Whether your child needs help with Theory of Knowledge, Extended Essay planning, or specific IB subject papers, we can match them with a tutor who knows the programme well.

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

Securing a place at Pate's Grammar School is one of the most competitive academic challenges a child in Gloucestershire can face. The school consistently ranks among the highest-performing state schools in England, and the 11+ selection process reflects that. The test assesses verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, mathematics, and English — and the standard required is genuinely high.

Many families in areas like Charlton Kings and Prestbury begin preparing their children well in advance of the Year 6 assessment, and rightly so. Familiarity with the question formats, timed practice, and targeted work on weaker areas all make a measurable difference. Leading Tuition builds structured preparation programmes that start from where your child actually is, rather than assuming a level of readiness they may not yet have reached.

Our 11+ preparation typically covers:

  • Verbal and non-verbal reasoning skills, including question types specific to the Gloucestershire 11+ format
  • Mathematics at and beyond the expected Year 6 curriculum level
  • English comprehension and writing, with attention to the style of passages used in selective assessments
  • Timed practice papers with structured feedback, so children learn to manage exam conditions as well as the content

We are honest with families from the start. Pate's is genuinely selective, and preparation alone cannot guarantee a place. What it can do is ensure your child performs to the very best of their ability on the day — and that is what we focus on.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

For sixth formers in Cheltenham with ambitions to study Medicine, Dentistry, or Veterinary Science, the admissions process extends well beyond A-Level grades. Most UK medical schools require the UCAT, and strong performance in this test is increasingly important as grade competition intensifies. Our tutors provide structured UCAT preparation, covering all five subtests with timed practice and technique-focused coaching.

We also support personal statement writing, interview preparation for medical school, and subject-specific tutoring for pupils applying to highly competitive courses at Oxford, Cambridge, and other leading universities. For pupils at Cheltenham Ladies' College or Cheltenham College, where Oxbridge applications are relatively common, we understand the additional preparation that these applications require and can support at every stage of the process.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Cheltenham

When should we start preparing for the Pate's Grammar School 11+?

Most families begin structured preparation around twelve to eighteen months before the assessment, typically in Year 4 or early Year 5. This allows enough time to build reasoning skills gradually rather than cramming, which rarely produces the best results in a test designed to assess genuine aptitude and developed ability.

Our child attends Cheltenham Bournside School and is following OCR for their GCSEs — can you match us with a tutor who knows that specification?

Yes. OCR is the specification our Cheltenham tutors work with most frequently, and we will always match your child with someone who knows the exact papers, assessment objectives, and mark scheme expectations for their subject. We do not assign tutors based on availability alone.

Do you offer tuition for pupils at Cheltenham Ladies' College or other independent schools in the area?

We do. Independent schools often have their own internal assessment schedules, scholarship examinations, and subject demands that sit alongside public qualifications. Our tutors are experienced working within those environments and can support both the school's own expectations and any external examinations your child is sitting.

We live in Bishops Cleeve — is that too far out for in-person sessions?

Not at all. We work with families across the wider Cheltenham area, including Bishops Cleeve and other villages and towns nearby. Where in-person sessions are not practical, online tuition is equally effective — and many of our families find a combination of both works best depending on the time of year and what their child is working towards.
