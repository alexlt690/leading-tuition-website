Reading is home to some of the most academically ambitious families in the South East, and the pressure on children here reflects that. Whether you're preparing a Year 5 or 6 child for the fiercely competitive 11+ entry to Reading School or Kendrick School — two of the highest-performing grammar schools in England — or supporting a sixth-former through A-Level examinations that will shape their university options, the academic stakes feel very real. At Leading Tuition, we work with families across Reading and the surrounding area, including Caversham, Earley, and Woodley, providing structured, expert tuition that gives children a genuine advantage.

Tutoring in Reading — What We Offer

Leading Tuition provides one-to-one tutoring for pupils from primary age through to university admissions level. Our tutors are subject specialists, not generalists, and every programme begins with a proper assessment of where your child is now and what they need to reach their goal. We work with the specific syllabuses, exam boards, and entry requirements that Reading families are actually dealing with — not a one-size-fits-all approach built for somewhere else.

Sessions are available online or in person, and we match each child with a tutor whose subject knowledge, teaching style, and experience align with that pupil's needs. Parents tell us they value the consistency — the same tutor, a clear plan, and regular communication so you always know how things are progressing.

GCSE and A-Level Support

The majority of secondary pupils in Reading sit AQA and OCR qualifications, and our tutors know these specifications in detail. Whether your child is working through OCR Gateway Science at GCSE, AQA English Language and Literature, or preparing for AQA Mathematics, we build sessions around the exact content, command words, and mark scheme expectations they will face.

At A-Level, the pressure intensifies. Students at schools including The Abbey School Reading and The Oratory School are often targeting highly competitive university courses, and the difference between a B and an A* can determine which doors open. Our A-Level tutors work closely with students on both content mastery and exam technique — understanding how to structure an argument under timed conditions, how to approach extended writing in the humanities, and how to approach the problem-solving questions that distinguish the top mark bands in mathematics and the sciences.

We support the following at GCSE and A-Level:

  • Mathematics and Further Mathematics (AQA and OCR)
  • Sciences — Biology, Chemistry, Physics (AQA and OCR)
  • English Language and English Literature (AQA)
  • History, Geography, and the Humanities (AQA and OCR)
  • Modern Foreign Languages including French, Spanish, and German
  • Economics, Business Studies, and Psychology

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

The 11+ in Reading is genuinely demanding. Entry to Reading School and Kendrick School is among the most competitive in the country, drawing applicants from across Berkshire and beyond. Both schools assess candidates in verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, mathematics, and English, and the standard required to secure a place is consistently high. Families in areas like Tilehurst and Henley-on-Thames often begin preparing their children in Year 4 or early Year 5, and for good reason — the gap between a well-prepared child and an unprepared one is significant by the time the test arrives.

Leading Tuition's 11+ programmes are structured around the specific format and difficulty level of the tests used by Reading's grammar schools. We begin with a diagnostic assessment to identify your child's current strengths and the areas that need the most attention. From there, we build a programme that develops reasoning skills progressively, rather than simply drilling past papers from the start. Timed practice, exam familiarity, and confidence under pressure all form part of the preparation — because performing on the day is as important as knowing the material.

We also support families considering St Joseph's College Reading and other independent schools with selective entry, where strong performance at assessment is equally important.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

For families with children applying to competitive university courses — Medicine, Law, Engineering, or Oxbridge — the admissions process extends well beyond A-Level grades. Leading Tuition supports students through the full range of admissions requirements, including UCAT preparation for Medicine applicants (now used by Oxford, Cambridge, and Imperial following the abolition of BMAT in 2023), personal statement development, and interview preparation.

Our tutors include graduates and postgraduates from Russell Group universities who understand what admissions tutors are looking for and how to help students present themselves with clarity and confidence. For a Reading sixth-former with the grades and the ambition, targeted preparation at this stage can make a decisive difference.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Reading

When should we start preparing for the 11+ entry to Reading School or Kendrick School?

Most families begin structured preparation in Year 4 or the autumn term of Year 5, which allows around 12 to 18 months of focused work before the tests. Starting earlier gives time to develop reasoning skills properly rather than rushing through content in the final weeks. That said, we do work with families who come to us later — a well-targeted programme in Year 6 can still make a meaningful difference.

My child is at a Reading secondary school and struggling with their OCR or AQA GCSEs — how quickly can tuition help?

It depends on where the gaps are and how much time remains before examinations, but most students see a noticeable improvement in confidence and performance within four to six weeks of consistent, well-targeted sessions. We begin with an honest assessment so we can focus time where it will have the greatest impact.

Do you offer tuition for pupils at The Abbey School Reading or The Oratory School?

Yes. We regularly support pupils at both schools, as well as other independent and state schools across Reading and Berkshire. Our tutors are familiar with the academic expectations at these schools and can align sessions with the specific syllabuses and internal assessment formats your child is working with.

Is online tuition as effective as in-person sessions for Reading pupils?

For the majority of subjects and age groups, online tuition works extremely well — particularly for GCSE and A-Level students who are comfortable working on screen. We use interactive tools that allow tutors and students to work through problems together in real time. Some families in areas like Caversham or Woodley prefer in-person sessions for younger children, and we can discuss what is likely to suit your child best when you get in touch.
