11+ and Grammar School Preparation

Buckinghamshire's 11+ process is one of the most competitive in England. The county assessment — which determines grammar school eligibility — tests verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, English, and Mathematics. The standard required for Aylesbury Grammar School and Sir Henry Floyd Grammar School is high, and children who succeed have typically had structured preparation that goes well beyond working through practice papers.

We begin 11+ preparation with an honest assessment of where your child currently stands, then build a programme that develops the reasoning skills, mathematical confidence, and English comprehension that the assessment targets. Preparation should ideally begin in Year 4 — not because younger is always better, but because the skills the 11+ tests are developed gradually, not memorised quickly.

Families in Milton Keynes sometimes also consider grammar schools in neighbouring counties — including those in Hertfordshire (Watford Grammars) or in the adjacent areas of Northamptonshire — and we can advise on the relevant assessments if that is relevant to your situation.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Milton Keynes

How does the Buckinghamshire 11+ work for Milton Keynes families?

Buckinghamshire is a fully selective county, meaning secondary school placement is determined by the 11+ result. Children sit the county assessment in September of Year 6, and those who reach the qualifying standard are eligible to apply to Buckinghamshire grammar schools. The process is managed by the county, but individual grammar schools also rank candidates, so achieving a high score — not just a passing score — matters significantly.

When should we start 11+ preparation for Aylesbury Grammar School?

We typically recommend beginning focused preparation in Year 4 or the start of Year 5. The 11+ tests reasoning skills that develop with sustained practice over time — not skills that can be acquired in a short burst before the assessment. Starting in Year 4 gives enough time to build these skills gradually without creating undue pressure on a primary-age child.

We are in Woburn Sands — do you cover our area?

Yes. We work with families across Milton Keynes and the surrounding area including Woburn Sands, Newport Pagnell, Bletchley, Wolverton, and Stony Stratford. Most sessions are conducted online, but in-person is available where preferred.

My son passed the 11+ and is now at Aylesbury Grammar — can you support him at GCSE?

Yes. Grammar school students frequently benefit from one-to-one support — the pace is high and the cohort is able, which can make it feel difficult to ask for help in class. We match students with tutors who know the specific AQA or OCR specification their school follows and can work through the topics where they are losing marks or confidence.
