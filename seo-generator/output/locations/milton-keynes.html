11+ and Grammar School Preparation

For families in Milton Keynes, the 11+ is one of the most significant academic hurdles their child will face. Buckinghamshire operates a fully selective system, which means that performance in the 11+ examination directly determines secondary school placement. The two grammar schools most commonly targeted by families in this area — Aylesbury Grammar School and Sir Henry Floyd Grammar School — are both highly competitive, and preparation needs to begin well in advance of the test, typically in Year 4 or early Year 5.

The Buckinghamshire 11+ assesses verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, and numerical reasoning. These are skills that can be developed with the right guidance, but they require consistent, structured practice rather than last-minute cramming. Our tutors work with children to build genuine fluency in these areas, helping them become comfortable with the format and timing of the test so that on the day itself, they can perform to their true ability.

We understand that this process can feel daunting for parents as well as children. Our approach is to keep families informed and involved at every stage, so you always have a clear picture of where your child stands and what the next steps are.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

For older students in Milton Keynes with ambitions to study Medicine, Dentistry, or other competitive courses at leading universities, the admissions process extends well beyond A-Level grades. Students applying to medical school will need to prepare for the UCAT (used by the majority of UK medical schools, including those at Oxford, Cambridge, and Imperial), and many will also face demanding personal statement requirements and multiple mini interviews (MMIs).

Our tutors include graduates and professionals with direct experience of these pathways. We provide structured UCAT preparation, personal statement guidance, and interview coaching — helping students present themselves as genuinely strong candidates, not just students with good grades. For those applying to Oxford or Cambridge, we also offer support with written work submissions and admissions tests specific to individual courses.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Milton Keynes

When should we start preparing for the Buckinghamshire 11+ if we live in Milton Keynes?

Most families begin structured preparation in Year 4 or at the start of Year 5, giving around 12 to 18 months before the test. This allows time to build genuine fluency in verbal and non-verbal reasoning rather than simply familiarising a child with the format at the last minute. Starting earlier also means there is less pressure on the child as the test date approaches.

My child attends Walton High School and is struggling with their AQA GCSE subjects — can you help?

Yes. Our tutors are familiar with the AQA specifications followed at schools like Walton High and can provide targeted support in specific subjects. Whether your child needs help with AQA GCSE Maths, Combined Science, or English Language, we will match them with a tutor who knows the exact content and assessment style they are working towards.

Do you offer in-person tuition in Milton Keynes, or is everything online?

We offer both. Many families across Milton Keynes — including those in areas like Newport Pagnell and Stony Stratford — find online tuition convenient and effective, particularly for secondary and sixth-form students. For younger children or those who benefit from face-to-face interaction, in-person sessions can also be arranged. We will discuss what works best for your child when you get in touch.

My child is applying to medical school from sixth form in Milton Keynes — do you cover UCAT preparation?

We do. UCAT preparation is one of the areas where early, structured practice makes a significant difference to scores. Our tutors work through each of the five UCAT subtests systematically, helping students develop both the skills and the time management strategies the test demands. We can also support with personal statements and interview preparation for medical school applications.
