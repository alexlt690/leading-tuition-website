GCSE and A-Level Support

Devon schools follow predominantly OCR specifications, and our tutors are matched to students based on their exact board and course. OCR's assessment structures in subjects like Biology, Chemistry, History, and English Literature differ meaningfully from AQA — understanding these differences is part of what makes targeted preparation more effective than generic revision.

Students at Exeter School — the city's leading day independent — are working towards results that open doors to highly competitive degree programmes. At A-Level, we support students across all subjects, with particular strength in the sciences, mathematics, English, and history. For students at Exeter College, one of the South West's largest sixth form colleges, individual support is particularly valuable in environments where class sizes can limit one-to-one time with teachers.

11+ and Independent School Preparation

Devon has no grammar schools, so secondary transfer here does not involve the 11+. However, families considering Exeter School or The Maynard School face competitive entry processes — both schools set their own entrance assessments in English and Mathematics, with the standard reflecting the academically ambitious environments they maintain.

We support children from Year 3 upwards with the foundational skills that underpin independent school entrance performance: mathematical fluency, reading comprehension, and written clarity. For families in Exeter considering boarding school options further afield, we also support preparation for Common Entrance and other assessment formats.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

The University of Exeter Medical School — now one of the UK's established medical schools — is a significant local aspiration for many of Exeter's sixth-formers. Leading Tuition supports students with UCAT preparation, personal statement development, MMI interview coaching, and broader application strategy. For students targeting other medical schools across the UK, or aiming at highly competitive degree programmes at Bristol, Durham, Edinburgh, or London universities, specialist admissions preparation is a practical necessity.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Exeter

My son is at Exeter College and struggling with A-Level Chemistry — can you help?

Yes. A-Level Chemistry is consistently one of the subjects where demand for one-to-one support is highest. The OCR specification that Exeter College typically follows has specific required practicals and a question style that rewards preparation targeted to that board. We match students with chemistry tutors who know the OCR specification thoroughly and can work through the areas where confidence is weakest.

Does Exeter School have a competitive entrance process?

Yes. Exeter School is academically selective and sets its own entrance papers at 11+. The assessment tests English and Mathematics to a standard that reflects the school's internal expectations. Children who are well prepared — with strong foundational skills and familiarity with the format — approach the assessment with genuine confidence.

We are in Topsham — do you cover our area?

Yes. We work with families across Exeter and the surrounding Devon area including Topsham, Heavitree, Pinhoe, Exwick, and Crediton. Most sessions are conducted online, but in-person is available where preferred.

Can you support a student applying to Exeter Medical School?

Yes. The University of Exeter uses UCAT and interview as key components of its selection process. We support students with UCAT preparation across all sections, personal statement development, and MMI-style interview coaching. Exeter Medical School is competitive — strong A-Level grades are necessary but not sufficient, and the UCAT and interview components require specific preparation.
