Private Tuition in Croydon

Expert tutors supporting families across Croydon

Book a Free Consultation

Croydon families navigating secondary school choices face a genuinely complex landscape. On one side, the borough's own independent schools — Whitgift School, Trinity School Croydon, and Old Palace of John Whitgift School — offer some of the strongest academic environments in South London, with competitive entry and demanding internal expectations. On the other, the neighbouring Sutton borough's grammar schools — Nonsuch High School for Girls, Wallington County Grammar School, and others — attract applicants from Purley, Coulsdon, and South Croydon in large numbers. At Leading Tuition, we support families through both routes and all the stages that follow.

Tutoring in Croydon — What We Offer

Leading Tuition provides one-to-one tuition for students from primary age through to A-Level and beyond. Our tutors are subject specialists matched to your child's exact school, syllabus, and goals. Whether your child attends Whitgift School, a Sutton grammar, Riddlesdown Collegiate, or another local school, we find the tutor who understands their specific course and examination board.

Sessions are available online and in person across Croydon, Addiscombe, Selsdon, Purley, Coulsdon, and the wider South London area. We schedule around school timetables and family commitments, including evenings and weekends.

  • 11+ preparation for Sutton grammar schools and Croydon independent school entrance
  • GCSE and A-Level support across all core and option subjects
  • Tutors experienced with AQA, Edexcel, and OCR specifications
  • Medicine and university admissions preparation including UCAT
  • Flexible online and in-person sessions

GCSE and A-Level Support

Students at Whitgift, Trinity, and Old Palace are working within high-attainment environments where internal expectations mirror those of the most selective schools in the country. Our tutors understand that these students need targeted, board-specific preparation — not generic revision — and they are matched accordingly.

Croydon schools follow predominantly AQA and Edexcel specifications, with some OCR. The differences between boards in mark scheme emphasis, question style, and assessment structure are significant enough to matter in exam preparation. We select tutors for their knowledge of specific boards, not just their subject expertise.

At GCSE, we support students across mathematics, the sciences, English language and literature, history, geography, economics, and languages. At A-Level, demand in Croydon concentrates in mathematics, the sciences, economics, English, and humanities — and for students at Whitgift and Trinity aiming for highly competitive universities, performing at the top of the grade boundaries is what their applications require.

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

The Sutton grammar schools — including Nonsuch High School for Girls, Wallington County Grammar School, Wallington High School for Girls, and Wilson's School — attract large numbers of applicants from Croydon's southern suburbs. The competition is intense, and children who perform well have typically had structured preparation that goes beyond working through practice papers. They know the format, have developed speed and accuracy under timed conditions, and have built the reasoning skills that the assessments specifically target.

For families considering the Whitgift Foundation schools, the entrance assessments have a different character — they assess academic potential and reasoning rather than following a local authority format. Our tutors are familiar with both routes and can advise on the best approach for your child's specific profile.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

Whitgift and Trinity have strong track records of students gaining entry to Russell Group and London universities. Leading Tuition supports sixth-form students with UCAT preparation for medicine applications, Oxbridge admissions test coaching, personal statement development, and interview preparation. For students aiming at the most competitive courses, this specialist preparation is what makes the difference between an application that stands out and one that does not.

Why Croydon Families Choose Leading Tuition

We understand the Croydon educational landscape — the independent schools, the grammar school routes across the borough boundary, and the pressures that families here are actually navigating. Every tutor recommendation we make is based on a genuine understanding of your child's school, their course, and their target outcomes. Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Croydon

Which grammar schools can Croydon children realistically apply to?

The main grammar school options for Croydon families are in the neighbouring London Borough of Sutton: Nonsuch High School for Girls, Wallington County Grammar School, Wallington High School for Girls, and Wilson's School for boys. Some families in South Croydon also consider schools in Surrey. We can advise on the specific assessments each school uses and help you make an informed decision about which to target.

My son is at Whitgift and finding A-Level Further Maths very difficult — can you help?

Yes. Further Maths A-Level is one of the hardest courses available, and even able students at strong schools regularly need one-to-one support to fully consolidate the content. We'll match your son with a tutor who knows the specific Edexcel or AQA Further Maths specification his school follows and can work through the topics where he is losing marks.

We are in Coulsdon — can you support 11+ preparation for the Sutton grammars?

Yes. Coulsdon is well placed for Sutton grammar applications, and we work with families throughout South Croydon, Coulsdon, and Purley on this. We typically recommend starting focused preparation in Year 4 or early Year 5 to give your child the best possible preparation without undue pressure.

Can you help with personal statement writing for university applications?

Yes. Personal statement support is one of our specialist areas. We work with students to develop a clear, honest, and compelling statement that reflects their genuine interest in their subject — one that reads naturally and withstands the scrutiny of admissions tutors at competitive universities.
