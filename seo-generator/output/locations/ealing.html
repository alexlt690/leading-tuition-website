Expert tutors supporting families across Ealing

Ealing is one of West London's most educationally ambitious boroughs. Families across Northfields, Acton, Hanwell, and Greenford are navigating a complex mix of strong state comprehensives, competitive independent schools, and the ever-present question of which secondary route gives their child the best chance of reaching a good university. There are no grammar schools in Ealing, but the independent school sector — led by St Benedict's School and Notting Hill and Ealing High School — is genuinely competitive, and the state comprehensives here have widely varying outcomes. At Leading Tuition, we support families across all of these routes.

Tutoring in Ealing — What We Offer

Leading Tuition provides one-to-one tuition from primary through to A-Level and university admissions support. Our tutors are subject specialists matched to your child's exact school, syllabus, and goals. Whether your child attends St Benedict's School, Notting Hill and Ealing High School, Drayton Manor High School, Greenford High School, or another local school, we find a tutor who understands their specific course and examination board.

Sessions are available online and in person across Ealing, Southall, Northfields, Acton, and the wider West London area.

  • Independent school entrance preparation for St Benedict's and Notting Hill and Ealing High
  • GCSE and A-Level support across all subjects
  • Tutors experienced with AQA and Edexcel specifications
  • Medicine and university admissions preparation
  • Flexible online and in-person sessions

GCSE and A-Level Support

Ealing's secondary schools follow predominantly AQA and Edexcel specifications, and our tutors are matched to students based on their exact board and course. The differences in question style and mark scheme emphasis between AQA and Edexcel are significant — especially in subjects like mathematics, sciences, and English — and preparation that ignores them is less effective.

At GCSE, we support students across all core and option subjects. At A-Level, demand in Ealing concentrates in the subjects where the content step-up is steepest: mathematics, further mathematics, the sciences, and economics. For students at St Benedict's or Notting Hill and Ealing High who are targeting competitive universities, performing at the top of the grade boundaries is increasingly what applications require.

11+ and Independent School Preparation

Ealing has no grammar schools, so secondary transfer here does not typically involve the 11+. However, families considering St Benedict's School or Notting Hill and Ealing High School (a GDST school) face competitive entrance assessments that test academic potential across verbal and non-verbal reasoning, English, and mathematics. These assessments have a different character from local authority 11+ tests, and preparation should reflect this.

We support children from Year 3 upwards with the foundational skills that underpin performance in independent school entrance assessments: reading fluency, mathematical confidence, and the ability to reason clearly under timed conditions. For families considering schools outside the borough — in Hammersmith, Kensington, or elsewhere in West London — we can advise on the specific assessments and what preparation is required.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

West London families frequently target London universities and competitive degree programmes. Leading Tuition supports sixth-form students with UCAT preparation for medicine applications, personal statement development, Oxbridge admissions coaching, and interview preparation. For students aiming at Imperial, UCL, King's, or any of the UK's leading medical schools, specialist admissions preparation is a practical necessity.

Our tutors are Oxford and Cambridge graduates selected for their subject expertise and their ability to communicate clearly with young people. Parents tell us that consistency, honest communication, and tutors who genuinely invest in their students' progress are what matter most — and those are the standards we hold ourselves to.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Ealing

Is there an 11+ in Ealing, or is it only for independent schools?

Ealing Borough does not operate a grammar school system, so there is no standard 11+ for state school placement here. However, independent schools such as St Benedict's and Notting Hill and Ealing High School set their own entrance assessments. Some families in Ealing also consider grammar schools in neighbouring boroughs or counties — if that is relevant to your situation, we can advise on what those assessments involve.

My son is at Drayton Manor and struggling with GCSE Sciences — can you help?

Yes. GCSE Sciences are a common area of demand, and the Combined Science or Triple Science decision at the start of Year 9 often sets expectations that students then find challenging to meet. We match students with science tutors who know the specific AQA or Edexcel specification their school follows and can work through the topics where they are losing marks or confidence.

We are in Southall — do you cover our area?

Yes. We work with families across Ealing, Southall, Hanwell, Acton, Northfields, and Greenford. Most sessions are online, but in-person is available where preferred.

Can you support a student applying to medicine from a West London school?

Yes. UCAT preparation, personal statement support, MMI interview coaching, and broader application strategy are all specialist areas for us. Students from West London frequently apply to Imperial College London School of Medicine, UCL, King's, and other highly competitive programmes — and the preparation required for those applications is specific and demanding.
