Manchester is one of the most educationally competitive cities in the UK. From the selective independent schools in the south of the city to the grammar schools drawing applicants from across Trafford, Altrincham, and Sale, the pressure on families to make the right academic choices — and to support their children through them — starts early and rarely lets up. At the same time, Manchester's proximity to one of the country's most prestigious universities, alongside strong demand for places at medical schools, means that the stakes at A-Level feel just as high. Leading Tuition works with families across Manchester to provide focused, expert support at every stage of that journey.

Tutoring in Manchester — What We Offer

We provide one-to-one private tuition for pupils from primary age through to sixth form and beyond. Our tutors are subject specialists, not generalists, and every student is matched carefully based on their specific needs, learning style, and goals. Whether your child needs help building confidence in a subject they find difficult, or wants to push for the highest grades in a subject they love, we tailor our approach accordingly.

Lessons take place online, allowing us to work with families across the city — from Didsbury and Chorlton in the south to Prestwich in the north, and across into Altrincham and Sale. We cover a wide range of subjects and levels, including:

  • Primary English and Maths, including 11+ preparation

  • UCAT preparation for medical school applicants
  • University admissions support, including personal statement guidance
  • Oxbridge preparation and interview coaching

GCSE and A-Level Support

The majority of state schools across Greater Manchester follow AQA specifications, which is the dominant exam board across the North West. Our tutors know these specifications in detail — the exact mark scheme expectations, the common pitfalls in AQA exam technique, and the areas where students most often lose marks unnecessarily. Whether your child is working through AQA GCSE Biology, AQA A-Level Chemistry, or AQA English Literature, their tutor will work directly from the relevant specification and past papers.

For students at independent schools such as Manchester Grammar School, Manchester High School for Girls, Withington Girls' School, or Cheadle Hulme School, the picture can be more varied. Some independent schools follow different exam boards or offer the Pre-U or IB alongside A-Levels. We take time to understand exactly what your child's school is teaching and how they are being assessed, so that our support fits seamlessly alongside their school programme rather than cutting across it.

GCSE and A-Level years are often the point at which parents first seek a tutor, frequently after a disappointing mock result or a growing sense that their child is falling behind. We would always encourage families not to wait for a crisis. Early, consistent support tends to produce far better outcomes than intensive last-minute intervention.

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

Competition for grammar school places in this part of Greater Manchester is intense. Altrincham Grammar School for Boys, Altrincham Grammar School for Girls, and Sale Grammar School are among the most sought-after state schools in the country, and the 11+ process that governs entry is demanding. These schools use the CEM (Centre for Education and Monitoring) assessment, which tests verbal reasoning, numerical reasoning, and reading comprehension in ways that reward genuine ability alongside careful preparation.

Many families begin preparing their children in Year 4 or early Year 5. This is not about drilling children to exhaustion — it is about familiarising them with the format of the test, building the underlying skills that the assessment draws on, and ensuring they can work accurately under timed conditions. Our tutors take a structured but unhurried approach, building a clear picture of each child's strengths and the areas that need more attention before working through a carefully planned programme of practice.

We also support families applying to selective independent schools, where entrance assessments and interviews form part of the admissions process. Schools such as Manchester Grammar School and Withington Girls' School have their own entrance requirements, and preparation for these benefits from the same careful, personalised approach.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

Manchester is home to one of the UK's leading medical schools, and the University of Manchester consistently attracts some of the most competitive applicants in the country. For sixth-form students with ambitions to study Medicine, Dentistry, or other highly competitive courses, the admissions process extends well beyond A-Level grades.

The UCAT (University Clinical Aptitude Test) is now used by Oxford, Cambridge, Imperial, and the vast majority of UK medical schools as a core part of their admissions process. It is a test that rewards focused preparation, and many students significantly improve their scores with structured practice. Our tutors are experienced in UCAT preparation and work with students on all five subtests, helping them develop both accuracy and the time management skills the test demands.

We also support students with personal statements, helping them articulate their motivations and experiences clearly and compellingly, and with interview preparation for medical school and Oxbridge candidates.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Manchester

When should we start preparing for the 11+ if we are aiming for Altrincham Grammar or Sale Grammar?

Most families begin structured preparation in Year 4 or the autumn term of Year 5, giving around twelve to eighteen months before the test. Starting earlier allows for a steadier pace and reduces the pressure on the child in the final weeks. That said, children who begin later can still make strong progress — the right starting point depends on where your child currently is and how much time is available.

My child's school follows AQA, but they are really struggling with the exam technique. Can a tutor help with that specifically?

Yes, and this is one of the most common reasons families contact us. Understanding the content of a GCSE or A-Level course is only part of what is needed — AQA mark schemes reward very specific ways of structuring answers, and many able students lose marks simply because they have not been shown what examiners are looking for. A tutor who knows the AQA specification well can make a significant difference here.

We live in Didsbury — do you have tutors who work in our area, or is everything online?

All of our tuition is delivered online, which means location is not a barrier. Families in Didsbury, Chorlton, Prestwich, Sale, and across Greater Manchester all access the same pool of specialist tutors. Many parents find that online lessons actually fit more easily into family life, removing travel time and allowing sessions to happen at times that suit the household.

Our daughter is applying to study Medicine at the University of Manchester. What support can you offer beyond A-Level tutoring?

We can support her with UCAT preparation, personal statement writing, and interview coaching. Medical school applications are highly competitive, and the non-academic elements of the process — the aptitude test, the personal statement, and the interview — are areas where focused preparation genuinely makes a difference. We would start by understanding where she is in the process and building a plan around her timeline and specific needs.
