The Royal Borough of Kingston upon Thames is home to two of the most academically competitive grammar schools in England. Tiffin School and Tiffin Girls' School consistently rank among the top state schools in the country by examination results, and the competition for a place at either school is intense — hundreds of children from across South West London and Surrey sit the assessment each year for a small number of available places. This creates a level of 11+ preparation pressure in Kingston and the surrounding areas that is among the highest anywhere in the country. At Leading Tuition, we understand this landscape and we support families through it.

Tutoring in Kingston upon Thames — What We Offer

Leading Tuition provides one-to-one tuition for students from primary age through to A-Level and beyond. Our tutors are subject specialists matched to students based on their exact school, syllabus, and needs. Whether your child attends Tiffin School, Tiffin Girls' School, Kingston Grammar School, Surbiton High School, or a local comprehensive, we will find the right tutor for them.

Sessions are available online and in person, giving families across Kingston, Surbiton, New Malden, Ham, and Tolworth the flexibility they need around school and family life.

  • 11+ preparation specifically calibrated for Tiffin School and Tiffin Girls' School
  • GCSE and A-Level support across all subjects
  • Oxbridge and medicine admissions preparation
  • Tutors experienced across AQA and OCR specifications
  • Online and in-person sessions — flexible scheduling including evenings and weekends

GCSE and A-Level Support

Students at Tiffin School and Tiffin Girls' School are working within one of the most academically demanding environments in the country. The internal expectations are high, the cohort is able, and the transition from being top of your primary class to being one of many equally capable students can be difficult to navigate. Our tutors understand this specific environment and provide the individual attention that even the most able students sometimes need to consolidate their understanding and maximise their performance.

Kingston schools follow predominantly AQA and OCR specifications. We match students to tutors who know their exact course — not tutors who know the subject generally but are unfamiliar with the specific question styles and mark scheme requirements of the board their child is sitting.

At A-Level, demand in Kingston concentrates in mathematics, further mathematics, the sciences, economics, and English — all subjects where the step up from GCSE is steep and where targeted support makes a measurable difference. Many Kingston students are targeting Oxbridge or competitive London universities, and for those students, performing at the top of the grade boundaries matters.

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

The 11+ for Tiffin School and Tiffin Girls' School is among the most demanding in the country. The assessments are rigorous, the competition is fierce, and children who perform well have typically been prepared in a way that goes beyond working through practice papers. They understand the format deeply, they have developed speed and accuracy under timed conditions, and they have worked on the reasoning skills — verbal and non-verbal — that these assessments specifically target.

Holy Cross School and Kingston Grammar School are independent options in the area, each with their own entrance assessment and a different character from the state grammar process. Families weighing up the options benefit from a tutor who understands both routes.

When we begin 11+ preparation with a family, we start with an honest assessment of where the child currently stands — not to discourage, but to build a programme that is realistic and effective. We typically begin formal preparation in Year 4 or early Year 5, giving enough time to develop the necessary skills without creating undue pressure.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

A significant proportion of students at both Tiffin schools go on to apply to Oxbridge, Russell Group medicine programmes, and other highly selective courses. Leading Tuition supports these students with UCAT preparation, personal statement development, Oxbridge admissions test coaching (MAT, LNAT, TSA, and others), and interview preparation. Many Kingston students are extremely capable academically — but admissions preparation requires specific knowledge that is different from subject knowledge, and that is where we add most value.

Why Kingston Families Choose Leading Tuition

Families in Kingston are informed and discerning. They have often researched the options thoroughly, and they understand that the quality of the tutor matters at least as much as the number of hours. Our tutors are Oxford and Cambridge graduates selected for their subject expertise, their ability to communicate clearly with young people, and their understanding of the specific schools and assessments that Kingston families care about.

We take the matching process seriously, and we communicate with parents honestly about what is realistic and what progress their child is making. That approach — straightforward, expert, and genuinely invested — is what Kingston families tell us they value most.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Kingston upon Thames

How early should we start preparing for the Tiffin 11+?

Most families who are seriously targeting Tiffin begin focused preparation in Year 4 or the start of Year 5. The assessment is genuinely demanding, and the children who perform well have typically had time to develop verbal and non-verbal reasoning skills properly — not just worked through papers in the final months. Starting earlier reduces pressure and allows preparation to be more gradual and effective.

My daughter is at Tiffin Girls' and finding A-Level Maths difficult. Is this common?

It is more common than most parents expect. A-Level Maths is genuinely hard — the jump from GCSE is steep — and being at a very good school does not make the content easier. Many able students at both Tiffin schools benefit from a tutor who can work through specific topics one-to-one, at their pace, without the pressure of a classroom environment.

We live in Surbiton — do you cover our area?

Yes. We work with families across Kingston, Surbiton, New Malden, Ham, Tolworth, and the wider Royal Borough. Most of our sessions are conducted online, which is often the most convenient option — but in-person tuition is available where preferred.

Can you support Oxbridge preparation for students at Tiffin Sixth Form?

Yes. Oxbridge preparation is one of our specialist areas. We support students with subject-specific admissions tests (MAT for Maths, LNAT for Law, TSA for PPE and others), personal statement development, and interview coaching. Students at Tiffin who are targeting Oxford or Cambridge benefit from tutors who have been through that process themselves and understand exactly what the admissions system is looking for.
