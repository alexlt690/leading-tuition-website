Tutoring in Portsmouth — What We Offer

Leading Tuition provides one-to-one tuition from primary through to sixth form and university admissions. GCSE and A-Level Support

Portsmouth schools follow predominantly AQA and OCR specifications, and our tutors are matched to students based on their exact board and course. At GCSE, we support students across all core and option subjects. At A-Level, demand in Portsmouth concentrates in the sciences, mathematics, English, and humanities — subjects where the step up from GCSE is steep and where board-specific preparation makes a measurable difference.

Students at Portsmouth Grammar School are working within a genuinely academically demanding environment — PGS is one of Hampshire's strongest schools by examination results, and the expectation is that students will achieve the grades needed for competitive university applications. One-to-one support helps these students consolidate understanding at the required depth and address specific weaknesses before they become persistent patterns.

11+ and Independent School Preparation

Portsmouth as an LEA does not operate a grammar school system, so the local authority 11+ is not a feature of the city's secondary transfer process. However, Portsmouth Grammar School is a selective independent that sets its own entrance assessments at 11+, and the standard required reflects its strong academic track record.

Some families in the Portsmouth area — particularly those in Fareham, Havant, and Waterlooville — also look to Hampshire's broader selective school landscape, and we can advise on preparation requirements for any schools you are considering. We support children from Year 3 upwards with the foundational skills that underpin independent school entrance performance.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

Portsmouth's proximity to Southampton — home to one of the UK's leading medical schools — creates strong local interest in medicine among the most able sixth-formers. Leading Tuition supports students with UCAT preparation, personal statement development, MMI interview coaching, and broader application strategy. The University of Southampton Medical School uses UCAT as a key selection tool, and preparation that goes beyond general study skills is essential for a competitive score.

We also support students targeting Exeter, Bristol, King's College London, and other medical schools that are realistic aspirations for Portsmouth's strongest A-Level students.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Portsmouth

Is Portsmouth Grammar School competitive at 11+?

Yes. Portsmouth Grammar School is academically selective and its 11+ entrance assessment tests English and Mathematics to a standard that reflects the school's ambition. Competition for places is meaningful, and children who are well prepared approach the assessment with genuine confidence. We support children from Year 3 upwards with the specific skills PGS's assessment requires.

My daughter is at PGS and considering medicine — what does she need to do?

A-Level subject choice is the first step — typically Biology, Chemistry, and a third subject. UCAT preparation typically begins in spring of Year 12. Personal statement development runs through the summer before Year 13. MMI interview coaching follows once shortlisting happens. We support all of these stages and can work with your daughter across each of them as a coherent process rather than isolated components.

We are in Fareham — do you cover our area?

Yes. We work with families across Portsmouth, Southsea, Gosport, Havant, Fareham, and Waterlooville. Most sessions are conducted online, but in-person is available where preferred.

My son is at Mayfield School and struggling with GCSE Chemistry — can you help?

Yes. GCSE Chemistry is one of the subjects where demand for tuition is consistently high, and OCR Chemistry in particular has specific required practicals and question styles that reward targeted preparation. We match students with tutors who know the exact specification their school follows and can work through the areas where understanding is weakest.
