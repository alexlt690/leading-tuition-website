The London Borough of Bromley has two of the most oversubscribed selective schools in Greater London, and the pressure this creates for local families is significant. Newstead Wood School for girls and St Olave's Grammar School for boys consistently rank among the top state schools in the country, and the competition for a place at either is intense — hundreds of children sit the assessment each year for a small number of available places. Families across Chislehurst, Petts Wood, Orpington, and Beckenham are acutely aware of what is at stake, and they plan accordingly. At Leading Tuition, we support families through this process and through everything that follows.

GCSE and A-Level Support

Students at Newstead Wood and St Olave's are working within some of the most demanding state school environments in the country. The cohort is highly able, expectations are high, and students who were top of the year at primary find themselves among peers who are equally capable. This transition — from being a very strong student at one level to being one of many very strong students — can be disorienting, and it is precisely where targeted one-to-one support adds the most value.

Bromley schools follow predominantly AQA and OCR specifications. Our tutors are matched to students based on their exact board and course — the mark scheme differences and question style variations between boards are significant, and preparation that ignores them is less effective. At GCSE and A-Level, we support students across all core and option subjects, with particular strength in the sciences, mathematics, English, and humanities.

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

The 11+ for Newstead Wood School and St Olave's Grammar School is among the most competitive in London. Both schools assess verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, English comprehension, and mathematics — but the specific format and weighting differ, and preparation should reflect this. Ravens Wood School is a third selective option for boys in the borough.

Children who perform well in these assessments have typically had structured, sustained preparation — not a last-minute push in Year 6, but a gradual building of skills that begins in Year 4 or early Year 5. Verbal and non-verbal reasoning skills can be developed with practice and teaching; the children who struggle are often those who encounter the format for the first time under exam conditions.

We begin 11+ preparation with an honest assessment of where your child currently stands, then build a programme that is realistic, effective, and — importantly — manageable alongside the rest of school and family life. We also prepare parents, keeping you informed throughout so you feel confident in the process.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

A significant proportion of Newstead Wood and St Olave's sixth-formers go on to apply to Oxbridge, Russell Group medicine, and other highly selective courses. Leading Tuition supports these students with UCAT preparation, personal statement development, Oxbridge admissions test coaching, and interview preparation. The admissions process for competitive courses is not well understood by most school teachers, and specialist support here makes a tangible difference to outcomes.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Bromley

How many children typically apply for a place at St Olave's Grammar School?

St Olave's receives several hundred applications each year for approximately 120 places — making it one of the most oversubscribed grammar schools in London. The assessment is rigorous and competitive, and children who succeed have typically been specifically and systematically prepared for it over a period of 12 to 18 months.

My daughter is in Year 4 and we are thinking about Newstead Wood. When should we start?

Year 4 is a good time to begin thinking about it and to start some light, skills-building work. By Year 5, preparation should be more structured — working on verbal and non-verbal reasoning, timed practice, and the English and mathematics components of the assessment. Starting now gives your daughter the time to develop skills gradually rather than cramming in the final months.

We are in Orpington — do you cover our area?

Yes. We work with families across the borough of Bromley including Orpington, Chislehurst, Petts Wood, Beckenham, Shortlands, and Bromley itself. Many sessions are conducted online, but in-person is available where preferred.

My son is at Ravens Wood and struggling with A-Level Physics — can you help?

Yes. A-Level Physics is one of the subjects where demand for tuition is consistently high — the content is challenging, and the mathematical demands are significant. We will match your son with a tutor who knows his exact specification (AQA or OCR Physics A/B) and can work through the areas where he is losing marks methodically and patiently.
