Tutoring in Derby — What We Offer

Leading Tuition provides one-to-one tuition from primary through to sixth form and university admissions. GCSE and A-Level Support

Derbyshire schools follow predominantly AQA and OCR specifications. Our tutors are matched to students based on their exact board and course. At GCSE, we support students across all core and option subjects. At A-Level, demand in Derby concentrates in the sciences, mathematics, English, and business — subjects where the step up from GCSE is steep and where a tutor who knows the specific examination board makes a meaningful difference.

Students at Derby Grammar School — a selective independent with strong academic results — are working in a demanding environment with high university expectations. At Ockbrook School, with its distinctive Moravian ethos and strong pastoral focus, students benefit from the individual attention that one-to-one tuition provides alongside an already supportive school environment.

11+ and Independent School Preparation

Derby has no state grammar schools within the city, so secondary transfer does not typically involve the local authority 11+. However, families considering Derby Grammar School or Ockbrook School face selective entry processes at 11+. Derby Grammar assesses Mathematics, English, and verbal and non-verbal reasoning, and the standard required is competitive.

We support children from Year 3 upwards with the foundational skills that underpin strong performance in independent school entrance assessments. For families in Derby considering grammar schools in neighbouring Staffordshire or Nottinghamshire, we can also advise on the specific assessments these schools use.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

The University of Nottingham and University of Leicester medical schools are the primary targets for Derby students aspiring to medicine — both are realistic aspirations for the most able local sixth-formers, and both are competitive. Leading Tuition supports students with UCAT preparation, personal statement development, MMI interview coaching, and broader application strategy. Growing demand from Derby families for this kind of specialist support reflects a broader trend of rising medical aspirations across the East Midlands.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Derby

My son is at Allestree Woodlands and finding A-Level Maths hard — can you help?

Yes. A-Level Maths is consistently one of the subjects where demand for one-to-one support is highest. The jump from GCSE is significant, and students who found GCSE Maths straightforward sometimes find A-Level genuinely challenging for the first time. We match students with maths tutors who know the specific AQA specification their school follows and can work through the content methodically.

Is there a grammar school 11+ in Derby?

No. Derby does not have state grammar schools, so secondary transfer does not involve the local authority 11+. However, the independent schools in the city — Derby Grammar School and Ockbrook School — set their own entrance assessments. Some families in Derby also consider grammar schools in neighbouring Staffordshire, and we can advise on preparation for those if relevant.

We are in Mickleover — do you cover our area?

Yes. We work with families across Derby, Mickleover, Allestree, Littleover, Chellaston, and Borrowash. Most sessions are conducted online, but in-person is available where preferred.

My daughter is in Year 12 and interested in applying to Nottingham Medical School — what preparation does she need?

Nottingham uses UCAT as a key component of its selection, alongside strong A-Level grades and a personal statement that demonstrates genuine commitment to medicine. UCAT preparation should typically begin in the spring or early summer of Year 12, alongside A-Level studies. We can support your daughter with UCAT coaching, personal statement development, and MMI interview preparation once offers start to arrive.
