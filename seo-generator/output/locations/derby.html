Derby is a city with a strong academic culture, and the demand for private tuition here reflects that. Families across the city — particularly in the suburbs of Littleover and Mickleover — are increasingly seeking structured, subject-specific support at GCSE and A-Level. Alongside this, there is growing interest among Derby families in medicine preparation, with many students setting their sights on the University of Nottingham and University of Leicester medical schools. Leading Tuition works with families across Derby to provide the kind of focused, expert support that makes a genuine difference to outcomes.

Tutoring in Derby — What We Offer

Leading Tuition provides one-to-one tuition for students from primary age through to sixth form and beyond. Our tutors are subject specialists, not generalists, and every student receives a personalised plan built around their specific school, syllabus, and goals. Whether your child is struggling to keep pace, aiming for top grades, or preparing for a competitive university application, we match them with a tutor who understands exactly what is required.

We work with students attending a wide range of Derby schools, including Derby Grammar School, Ockbrook School, Emmanuel School Derby, Allestree Woodlands School, and Merrill Academy. Because our tutors are familiar with the expectations and teaching styles at these schools, they can complement classroom learning rather than simply repeat it.

We support students across Derby's key residential areas, including Allestree, Chellaston, and Borrowash, with tuition delivered online so that geography is never a barrier to finding the right tutor.

GCSE and A-Level Support

The majority of Derby secondary schools follow AQA and OCR specifications, and our tutors are thoroughly familiar with both. This matters because the mark schemes, assessment objectives, and question styles vary significantly between boards — and a tutor who knows the specific specification your child is sitting can target their preparation far more precisely.

At GCSE, we see consistent demand for support in Maths, English Language, English Literature, the sciences, and History. At A-Level, subjects such as Chemistry, Biology, Maths, and Psychology are among the most commonly requested. Our tutors work directly from the relevant AQA or OCR syllabus, ensuring that every session is purposeful and exam-focused.

Some of the areas where our GCSE and A-Level students typically benefit most include:

  • Breaking down complex topics that have not clicked in the classroom
  • Developing exam technique and structured written responses
  • Working through past papers with detailed, mark-scheme-aligned feedback
  • Building confidence ahead of mock and final examinations

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

Derby does not operate a grammar school system, so the 11+ examination is not a significant feature of the local education landscape in the way it is in some neighbouring counties. However, this does not mean that primary-age preparation is any less important. Leading Tuition supports younger students — typically in Years 4 to 7 — with foundational work in Maths and English that builds the skills and habits they will need as they move into secondary school.

For families considering independent schools such as Derby Grammar School or Ockbrook School, we can also help students prepare for entrance assessments, which often test reasoning, comprehension, and mathematical ability at a level above the standard curriculum. Early preparation for these assessments gives students the best possible chance of a confident performance.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

There is a well-established tradition of academic ambition in Derby, and an increasing number of sixth-form students are applying to competitive medical programmes. The University of Nottingham and the University of Leicester are both popular targets for Derby students, and both require strong UCAT scores alongside excellent A-Level grades — typically including Chemistry and at least one other science.

Leading Tuition provides dedicated UCAT preparation, working through each of the five subtests systematically and helping students develop the speed and accuracy the test demands. We also support students with personal statement guidance and interview preparation, including the structured thinking required for Multiple Mini Interviews. For students applying to Oxford or Cambridge, we offer tailored support for their respective admissions processes, including subject-specific tests and interview technique.

Starting this preparation early — ideally in Year 12 — gives students the time to build genuine competence rather than rushing through content in the weeks before the test window opens.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Derby

My child attends Allestree Woodlands School and is struggling with AQA GCSE Science. Can you help?

Yes. We regularly support students following AQA Combined Science and Triple Science specifications. Our tutors work directly from the AQA syllabus and can address specific topics, help with required practicals understanding, and build exam technique for the structured questions that appear in the papers.

We live in Mickleover and are considering Derby Grammar School for our son. How early should we start entrance preparation?

We generally recommend beginning at least six months before the assessment date, which for most independent schools means starting in Year 5 or early Year 6. This allows time to identify any gaps, build reasoning skills steadily, and ensure your son is comfortable with the format and pace of the assessment.

My daughter is in Year 12 at Emmanuel School Derby and wants to apply to Nottingham Medical School. Where should she start?

The most important early step is registering for the UCAT, which opens in the spring of Year 12. We would recommend beginning structured UCAT preparation in the summer term so she has several months of practice before the test window. Alongside this, we can support her A-Level Chemistry and Biology to ensure her predicted grades are as strong as possible.

Do your tutors understand the OCR A-Level specifications, or mainly AQA?

Our tutors work across both AQA and OCR at GCSE and A-Level. When we match a tutor to your child, we confirm the exact specification they are following — including the year of the syllabus — so that the tutor is working from the correct content and assessment objectives from the very first session.
