GCSE and A-Level Support

London students sit GCSEs and A-Levels across a wide range of exam boards, and the difference between specifications matters. A student at one school may be studying Edexcel Mathematics while a friend nearby sits AQA — and the question styles, mark schemes, and coursework requirements differ meaningfully between them. Our tutors are trained to work within the exact specification your child is following, so no lesson time is wasted on irrelevant content.

At GCSE, we support students who are aiming for strong grades across core subjects as well as those targeting the top marks needed to secure places at competitive sixth forms, including the City of London School and North London Collegiate School. At A-Level, we work with students who need to build genuine subject mastery — not just exam technique — because universities, and particularly selective ones, can tell the difference. Whether your child is studying OCR Biology, AQA History, or Edexcel Economics, we have a specialist who knows that course in depth.

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

The 11+ in London is among the most competitive in the country. Grammar schools such as Henrietta Barnett School, Queen Elizabeth's School Barnet, and Tiffin Girls' School receive far more applications than they have places, and the children who succeed are typically those who have prepared thoroughly, strategically, and well in advance.

The 11+ tests vary by school and consortium. Most London grammar schools assess students in verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, mathematics, and English comprehension, though the format, timing, and weighting differ between institutions. Some schools use the GL Assessment, others use their own bespoke papers, and a small number participate in consortium arrangements. Understanding which tests your child will face — and preparing specifically for those — is essential.

Leading Tuition begins working with families from Year 4 or Year 5, building the core skills and confidence that underpin strong performance. Our tutors do not simply drill past papers; they identify the specific areas where each child needs to develop, whether that is speed and accuracy in maths, vocabulary breadth for verbal reasoning, or structured comprehension technique. We also help parents understand the process, the timeline, and what realistic preparation looks like — because navigating the 11+ for the first time can feel overwhelming.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

Each year, a significant number of London's sixth-form students apply to study Medicine, and competition for places at leading medical schools is intense. Students applying through UCAS must now demonstrate strong performance in the UCAT (University Clinical Aptitude Test), which is used by the majority of UK medical schools, including those at Oxford, Cambridge, and Imperial College London. Leading Tuition offers structured UCAT preparation that goes well beyond familiarisation — we help students develop the cognitive strategies and timed practice habits that translate into genuinely competitive scores.

Beyond Medicine, we support students applying to Oxbridge and Russell Group universities across all subjects. This includes personal statement guidance, subject-specific admissions test preparation — such as the MAT for Mathematics at Oxford or the LNAT for Law — and interview coaching that helps students articulate their thinking clearly and confidently under pressure.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in London

How early should we start preparing for the 11+ if we are targeting Henrietta Barnett or Queen Elizabeth's School Barnet?

Most families who are seriously targeting these schools begin structured preparation in Year 4 or early Year 5, giving roughly 12 to 18 months before the autumn sitting. Starting earlier allows time to build genuine skills rather than simply rehearsing past papers under pressure. That said, we have supported students who began later and still achieved strong results — the right starting point depends on your child's current level and the schools you are targeting.

My child's school uses Edexcel, but I have seen tutors advertising AQA resources — does the exam board really matter?

It matters more than many parents realise. The content across boards overlaps significantly, but the question styles, assessment objectives, and mark scheme language differ in ways that affect how students should structure their answers. A tutor who knows your child's exact specification will make every session more efficient and more directly useful for the exams your child will actually sit.

Do you offer in-person tutoring in areas like Hampstead or Richmond, or is everything online?

We offer both. Many of our London families choose online tuition for the flexibility it provides — particularly useful given the demands of school schedules and London commutes — but we do also arrange in-person sessions depending on location and tutor availability. We will always discuss the options with you and find an arrangement that works practically for your family.

Our daughter is applying to medical school and needs UCAT preparation — when should she start, and what does your support involve?

Most students sit the UCAT in the summer between Year 12 and Year 13, so preparation typically begins in the spring or early summer of Year 12. Our support involves an initial diagnostic assessment to identify which of the five UCAT subtests need the most attention, followed by structured sessions that build both the underlying cognitive skills and timed exam technique. We also help students understand how their UCAT score interacts with their predicted grades and personal statement when shortlisting decisions are made.
