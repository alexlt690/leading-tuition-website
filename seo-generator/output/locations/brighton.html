Private Tuition in Brighton

Expert tutors supporting families across Brighton

Brighton has always been a city with high academic ambitions. Whether your child is preparing for GCSEs at Dorothy Stringer School, navigating the demanding A-Level curriculum at BHASVIC or Varndean Sixth Form College, or sitting entrance assessments for Brighton College or Brighton and Hove High School, the pressure to perform is real — and it starts earlier than most parents expect. At Leading Tuition, we work with families across Brighton and the surrounding areas, including Hove, Kemptown, Fiveways, and Preston Park, to provide focused, expert private tuition that gives students the confidence and skills to reach their potential. Demand for A-Level support is particularly strong among students targeting Russell Group universities and competitive London institutions, and our tutors understand exactly what those applications require.

Tutoring in Brighton — What We Offer

Leading Tuition provides one-to-one private tuition for students from primary age through to university preparation. Our tutors are subject specialists, not generalists, and every student is matched carefully based on their specific needs, learning style, and academic goals. Sessions are available online or in person, and we work around school timetables and family life — because we know how busy Brighton families are.

We support students across a wide range of subjects and stages, including:

  • Primary and early secondary core subjects — English, Maths, and Science
  • GCSE preparation across all major subjects
  • A-Level tuition in sciences, humanities, and social sciences
  • University admissions preparation, including personal statement support and admissions tests
  • Independent school entrance assessments and interview preparation

We are experienced with the syllabuses used by Brighton's schools. Edexcel and OCR are the dominant exam boards across many secondary schools and sixth forms in Sussex, and our tutors know these specifications in detail — not just the content, but the mark schemes, the command words, and the specific ways examiners reward strong answers.

GCSE and A-Level Support

For GCSE students, the jump in difficulty from Key Stage 3 can catch families off guard. A student who coasted through Year 9 may suddenly find Year 10 far more demanding, particularly in subjects like Chemistry, History, or Mathematics where the OCR and Edexcel specifications require genuine depth of understanding rather than surface recall. Our tutors work through the material systematically, identify gaps early, and build the kind of exam technique that translates directly into marks.

At A-Level, the stakes are higher and the margin for error is smaller. Students at BHASVIC, Varndean, and Brighton College are often applying to highly competitive universities, and a single grade below expectation can affect their options significantly. Our A-Level tutors are typically graduates or postgraduates in their subject, many with direct experience of the universities their students are targeting. We focus not just on content, but on the analytical and evaluative skills that distinguish a B from an A or A*.

We also support students who have fallen behind after illness, disruption, or a difficult transition between schools — circumstances that are more common than many parents realise, and nothing to be embarrassed about.

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

Brighton and Hove does not have grammar schools, so the 11+ is not the defining pressure it is in some other parts of the country. However, a number of Brighton families do consider independent schools with their own entrance assessments, including Brighton College and Brighton and Hove High School, both of which select on the basis of academic ability and potential. Leading Tuition supports primary-age students and those in early secondary with the core skills — reading comprehension, written expression, and mathematical reasoning — that underpin strong performance in any entrance process.

For younger students, we also provide general academic support to build solid foundations before the demands of secondary school begin. Getting the basics right at Year 5 or Year 6 makes a meaningful difference to how a child approaches Year 7 and beyond.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

Brighton produces a significant number of students applying to medicine, dentistry, and other highly competitive degree programmes each year. The University of Sussex and Brighton's strong sixth form colleges send students to medical schools across the country, and the admissions process is rigorous. Leading Tuition offers structured preparation for the UCAT, which is now used by Oxford, Cambridge, Imperial, and the majority of UK medical schools as part of their admissions process.

Beyond admissions tests, we support students with personal statement writing, interview preparation, and the broader academic strengthening that competitive applications demand. A strong personal statement requires genuine subject knowledge and the ability to articulate it clearly — skills that benefit from expert guidance, not just a template.

We also work with students applying to competitive non-medical programmes at Russell Group universities, where subject-specific preparation and a well-constructed personal statement can make a real difference.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Brighton

My child is at BHASVIC and struggling with their A-Level choices — is it too late to get help partway through Year 12?

It is rarely too late to make a meaningful difference. Year 12 is actually an ideal time to intervene, because there is still enough time before the final A-Level exams to address gaps properly. We would begin with a clear assessment of where your child is and build a structured plan from there.

Do your tutors know the specific exam boards used by Brighton schools?

Yes. We are familiar with the Edexcel and OCR specifications that are most commonly used across Brighton and Hove secondary schools and sixth forms. Our tutors work from the actual specification documents and past papers, so the support is directly relevant to what your child will be assessed on.

We live in Hove — can we access in-person tuition, or is everything online?

We offer both. Many of our Brighton and Hove families choose online tuition for the flexibility it provides, but in-person sessions are available depending on the tutor and location. We will always discuss what works best for your family when we speak.

My daughter is in Year 6 and we are considering Brighton College at 11 — how early should we start preparing?

Starting in Year 5 gives a comfortable amount of time to strengthen the core skills that entrance assessments test, without putting undue pressure on a young child. We focus on building genuine ability rather than drilling to a test, which tends to produce better results and a more confident student on the day.
