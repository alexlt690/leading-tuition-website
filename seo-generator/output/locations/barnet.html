Queen Elizabeth's School Barnet — known locally as QEB — and Henrietta Barnett School attract applicants from across North London and Hertfordshire, and competition for their limited places is fierce. At Leading Tuition, we support families through every stage of this process.

GCSE and A-Level Support

Students at QEB and Henrietta Barnett are working within two of the most demanding state school environments in the country. The cohorts are highly able, the internal pace is fast, and students who were consistently top of the class at primary sometimes find themselves needing to work harder than they have before. One-to-one support provides the individual attention that allows a student to consolidate understanding at their own pace — something that even the best schools cannot always offer in a classroom setting.

Barnet schools follow predominantly AQA specifications. Our tutors are matched to students based on their exact board and course. At GCSE and A-Level, we support students across all core subjects — mathematics, further mathematics, the sciences, English, history, geography, economics, and languages — with particular experience of the content and mark scheme requirements of the courses QEB and Henrietta Barnett students are sitting.

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

The 11+ for Queen Elizabeth's School Barnet and Henrietta Barnett School is among the most competitive in London. QEB offers approximately 180 places each year and regularly receives over 2,000 first-choice applications; Henrietta Barnett is similarly oversubscribed. The assessments test verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, English comprehension, and mathematics — but understanding exactly how each school structures its assessment is important, as the weighting and format differ.

Children who perform at the top of these assessments have typically had structured preparation beginning in Year 4 or early Year 5. This is not about drilling — it is about genuinely developing the reasoning skills, reading fluency, and mathematical confidence that the assessments reward. A child who approaches the 11+ with well-developed underlying skills will perform more consistently than one who has simply memorised practice paper formats.

We begin with an honest assessment of your child's current level, then build a programme that is systematic, manageable alongside school, and calibrated to the specific schools you are targeting.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

A significant proportion of QEB and Henrietta Barnett sixth-formers target Oxbridge, medicine, and other highly competitive degree programmes. Leading Tuition supports these students with UCAT preparation, Oxbridge admissions test coaching (MAT, LNAT, TSA, and others), personal statement development, and interview preparation. The admissions process for these courses requires specialist knowledge that most school teachers — however excellent — do not have, and this is where expert support adds the most value.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Barnet

How many applications does Queen Elizabeth's School Barnet receive each year?

QEB regularly receives upwards of 2,000 first-choice applications for approximately 180 places, making it one of the most oversubscribed grammar schools in the country. Children who are successful have typically had structured, sustained preparation — not a last-minute push — and approach the assessment with well-developed reasoning skills and timed practice experience.

My daughter is in Year 5 and we are aiming for Henrietta Barnett. Is it too late to start?

Year 5 is a good time to be starting focused preparation. The 11+ is typically sat in September or October of Year 6, so there is around 12 to 18 months of preparation time from Year 5. That is a workable window if preparation is structured and consistent. We would begin with an assessment of where your daughter currently stands and build a programme from there.

We are in East Barnet — can you support us for both QEB and Haberdashers'?

Yes. Haberdashers' Boys' School and QEB have different entrance assessments with different formats and emphases. We are familiar with both and can advise on the most effective preparation strategy for a child targeting either or both, alongside one-to-one preparation sessions.

My son is at QEB and struggling with A-Level Maths despite being very able. Is this common?

Very common. QEB moves quickly and the A-Level content is genuinely hard. Being highly able does not make the material easy — it just means a student picks it up more quickly when it is explained well. A tutor who knows the AQA specification and works through the specific topics where your son is losing confidence can make a significant difference in a short space of time.
