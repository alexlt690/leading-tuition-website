GCSE and A-Level Support

York's secondary schools follow predominantly AQA specifications, which dominate across Yorkshire. Our tutors are matched to students based on their exact board and course — AQA's question styles and mark scheme emphases are specific, and preparation that is calibrated to them is more effective than general revision.

At GCSE, we support students across all core and option subjects. At A-Level, demand in York concentrates in the sciences, mathematics, English, and history — the subjects where the step up from GCSE is steepest and where a tutor who knows the specific content and assessment format makes a tangible difference.

Students at Bootham School and St Peter's School York are working within academically strong independent environments where university expectations are high. One-to-one support helps these students consolidate understanding at the level of depth their target universities expect — going beyond what even a good classroom teacher can offer to every student individually.

11+ and Independent School Preparation

York does not operate a grammar school system, so secondary transfer is not defined by the 11+. However, families considering Bootham School, St Peter's School York, or The Mount School York face competitive entry processes. Each school sets its own entrance assessments — testing English, Mathematics, and verbal reasoning — and the standard required reflects the academic environment these schools maintain.

We support children from Year 3 upwards with the foundational skills that underpin performance in independent school entrance assessments: mathematical fluency, reading comprehension, and the ability to reason and write clearly. For families in York considering boarding school options beyond the city, we can also support preparation for Common Entrance and other assessment formats.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

A growing number of York's sixth-form students are targeting medicine and other highly competitive degree programmes. The University of York itself is a respected destination, but many of the city's most ambitious students are aiming higher — at Leeds, Edinburgh, Durham, or the London medical schools. Leading Tuition supports these students with UCAT preparation, personal statement development, MMI interview coaching, and broader application strategy. We also support students targeting Oxbridge with admissions test preparation and interview coaching.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in York

My daughter attends Bootham School and is finding A-Level Biology difficult — can you help?

Yes. A-Level Biology is one of the subjects where demand for one-to-one support is most consistent. The content volume is large, the detail is exacting, and the jump from GCSE is significant. We match students with tutors who know the specific AQA or OCR specification their school follows and can work through the areas of weakness methodically and patiently.

Is there an 11+ process in York for state schools?

No. York's state secondary schools are comprehensive, and secondary transfer does not involve the 11+. However, families considering the city's independent schools — Bootham, St Peter's, The Mount — will encounter entrance assessments at 11+ or 13+, and preparation for these is something we support.

We are in Poppleton — do you cover our area?

Yes. We work with families across York and the surrounding North Yorkshire area including Poppleton, Bishopthorpe, Fulford, Heslington, and Clifton. Most sessions are conducted online, but in-person is available where preferred.

My son is in Year 12 and wants to apply to medicine — when should he start UCAT preparation?

UCAT is sat in the summer before Year 13 (typically July to September). Most students begin preparation in the spring or early summer of Year 12, once A-Level pressures allow. Starting earlier than this is not always better — focused preparation over 8 to 12 weeks is generally more effective than spreading it too thin over many months. We can advise on the right timeline for your son's specific situation.
