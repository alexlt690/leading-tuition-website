Private Tuition in York

Expert tutors supporting families across York

Book a Free Consultation

York is a city with serious academic ambitions. The presence of the University of York — a founding member of the 1994 Group and consistently ranked among the UK's leading research universities — shapes the expectations of families across the city and its surrounding villages. Whether your child attends St Peter's School York, Archbishop Holgate's School, or Fulford School, the pressure to perform at GCSE and A-Level is real, and the competition for places at Russell Group universities is fierce. We see growing numbers of York families seeking specialist support for A-Level subjects, and a marked rise in students preparing for medicine — including UCAT coaching and personal statement guidance. Leading Tuition works with families across York and the surrounding areas, including Heslington, Fulford, and Bishopthorpe, providing the kind of focused, expert tuition that makes a measurable difference when it matters most.

Expert Tutors in York — How We're Different

The tutors we place with York families are not generalists. Every tutor on our books has been rigorously vetted — not just for subject knowledge, but for their ability to explain clearly, adapt to a student's learning style, and build the kind of working relationship that keeps a young person engaged over months, not just weeks. Many of our tutors hold postgraduate qualifications or have professional experience in their field. When a student at Bootham School is preparing for Oxford interviews, or a Year 11 pupil in Clifton is struggling with AQA Chemistry, we match them with someone who genuinely knows that territory.

We also understand that parents in York are busy. Our sessions are delivered online, which means no commuting across the city and no disruption to your family's schedule. Every tutor works to a clear plan, and we keep parents informed throughout — so you always know what your child is working on and why.

Medicine, Oxbridge, and University Admissions Coaching

York produces a steady stream of students applying to the most competitive university courses in the country. Medicine, in particular, requires preparation that goes well beyond strong A-Level grades. The UCAT — the University Clinical Aptitude Test, now used by Oxford, Cambridge, Imperial, and the majority of UK medical schools — demands specific, structured practice. Our tutors have guided students through every stage of the medical application process: UCAT strategy, personal statements, and mock interviews that reflect what admissions panels are actually looking for.

For Oxbridge applicants, we offer subject-specific coaching that addresses the style of thinking these universities reward. Oxford and Cambridge interviews are not about reciting facts — they test how a student reasons under pressure. Our tutors are experienced in preparing students for exactly this, helping them develop the intellectual confidence to perform at their best.

A-Level and GCSE Tuition in York

The majority of York's secondary schools — including Archbishop Holgate's School and Fulford School — follow AQA specifications at both GCSE and A-Level. Our tutors are thoroughly familiar with AQA mark schemes, assessment objectives, and the specific demands of each subject at each tier. We also support students following Edexcel and OCR specifications where schools use them.

The subjects York families most frequently ask us about include:

  • A-Level Mathematics and Further Mathematics
  • A-Level Biology, Chemistry, and Physics
  • GCSE English Language and English Literature
  • A-Level History and Economics
  • GCSE and A-Level French, Spanish, and German

Whether your child needs help consolidating the foundations in Year 10 or is pushing for top grades in their final A-Level year, we build a programme around their specific syllabus, their school's teaching approach, and the timeline of their exams. We do not use off-the-shelf lesson plans — every session is tailored.

Primary, 11+, and Early Secondary Support

York does not operate a grammar school system, so there is no city-wide 11+ examination for families to navigate. However, independent schools such as St Peter's School York and The Mount School York have their own entrance assessments, and some families in the York area do consider selective schools further afield. We support primary-age children with core literacy and numeracy skills, building strong foundations that serve them well into secondary school. For Year 7 and Year 8 students making the transition to secondary, early support in English and Maths can make a significant difference to confidence and attainment before GCSE options are chosen.

Common Questions from York Parents

How do you match my child with the right tutor?

We take the matching process seriously. Before we recommend anyone, we find out about your child's current school, the specific syllabus they are following, their learning style, and what has or hasn't worked for them in the past. We then identify tutors with directly relevant experience and, where possible, give you the opportunity to meet the tutor before committing. If the match isn't right, we will find someone better — it is that straightforward.

Can you help with UCAT preparation for medical school applications?

Yes. UCAT preparation is one of our most requested services for York students. Our tutors work through each of the five test sections — Verbal Reasoning, Decision Making, Quantitative Reasoning, Abstract Reasoning, and Situational Judgement — with structured practice and timed conditions. We also help students understand how their UCAT score interacts with their A-Level predictions and personal statement when shortlisting decisions are made.

My child hasn't chosen their A-Level subjects yet — can you advise?

We can help you think through the implications of different subject combinations, particularly where a student has a specific university course or career in mind. Certain routes — medicine, engineering, economics — have prerequisites that are worth understanding early. We are happy to have that conversation with you before tuition begins.

My child is already doing well at school. Is tuition still worthwhile?

For many of the students we work with, the goal is not to catch up — it is to go further. A student achieving Bs who wants As, or a strong A-Level student aiming for a highly competitive university place, can benefit enormously from working with a specialist tutor who pushes their thinking and helps them perform at the top of their ability. Some of our most rewarding work is with students who are already capable but want to make the most of that capability.
