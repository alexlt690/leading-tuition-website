Harrow is one of North West London's most distinctive educational environments. The presence of Harrow School — one of England's most prestigious boarding schools — alongside its academic day school counterpart, John Lyon School, sets a tone of high expectation that permeates the whole borough. Families in Stanmore, Pinner, and Hatch End are acutely aware of what strong academic outcomes look like and are motivated to secure them for their children. At Leading Tuition, we support Harrow families at every stage of their child's education, from primary through to sixth form and university admissions.

Tutoring in Harrow — What We Offer

Leading Tuition provides one-to-one tuition for students from primary through to A-Level and beyond. Our tutors are subject specialists matched to your child's exact school, syllabus, and goals. Whether your child attends Harrow School, John Lyon School, St Dominic's Sixth Form College, Canons High School, or another local school, we find the tutor who understands their specific course and needs.

Sessions are available online and in person across Harrow, North Harrow, Rayners Lane, Stanmore, Pinner, and Hatch End.

  • Independent school entrance preparation for Harrow School and John Lyon School
  • GCSE and A-Level support across all subjects
  • Tutors experienced with AQA and Edexcel specifications
  • Medicine and Oxbridge admissions preparation
  • Flexible online and in-person sessions

GCSE and A-Level Support

Harrow's schools follow predominantly AQA and Edexcel specifications. John Lyon School in particular prepares students for highly competitive university applications, and the internal expectations are high. Students at St Dominic's Sixth Form College — a well-regarded Catholic sixth form with a tradition of strong results — also frequently benefit from one-to-one support to consolidate their understanding and build examination technique.

At GCSE, we support students across all core and option subjects. At A-Level, demand in Harrow concentrates in mathematics, the sciences, economics, and English — subjects where the content step-up is steep and where an expert tutor who knows the specific board and syllabus makes a measurable difference.

11+ and Independent School Preparation

Harrow Borough has no grammar schools, but the independent school entrance process is a significant feature of the local educational landscape. Entry to Harrow School is competitive and selective, with candidates sitting Common Entrance at 13+ alongside the school's own assessment. John Lyon School has a selective entry process at 11+, assessing mathematical reasoning, verbal reasoning, and English comprehension.

We support children preparing for independent school entrance from Year 3 upwards, building the foundational skills — mathematical fluency, reading comprehension, and the ability to reason clearly under timed conditions — that underpin performance in these assessments. For families considering 13+ entry to Harrow School itself, we support students through the Common Entrance preparation and the subject-specific interview process.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

Families in Harrow frequently target Russell Group universities and competitive degree programmes. Leading Tuition supports sixth-form students with UCAT preparation for medicine applications, Oxbridge admissions coaching, personal statement development, and interview preparation. For students aiming at Imperial College, UCL, or any of the UK's leading medical schools, this specialist preparation is a practical necessity alongside strong A-Level grades.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Harrow

Does John Lyon School have a competitive 11+ process?

Yes. John Lyon is selective and academically focused, with an entrance assessment that tests verbal and non-verbal reasoning, mathematics, and English. It draws applicants from across North West London, and the children who gain places have typically been well prepared. Starting preparation in Year 4 or early Year 5 gives enough time to develop the required skills properly.

My daughter is at a Harrow comprehensive and aiming for medicine — can you help?

Yes. A-Level subject choice, UCAT preparation, personal statement writing, and MMI interview coaching are all areas we support. We work with students from all school backgrounds who have the academic profile and motivation to pursue a medicine application, and we support them through every stage of that process.

We are in Pinner — do you cover our area?

Yes. We work with families across Harrow, Pinner, Stanmore, Hatch End, North Harrow, and Rayners Lane. Most sessions are conducted online, but in-person is available where preferred.

Can you help a student prepare for the 13+ Common Entrance for Harrow School?

Yes. Common Entrance at 13+ requires strong subject knowledge across a range of papers, and the preparation needed is different from GCSE preparation — it is more subject-broad and less focused on a single exam board specification. We work with students in Years 7 and 8 on the subjects where they need the most support, calibrated to the specific Common Entrance requirements.
