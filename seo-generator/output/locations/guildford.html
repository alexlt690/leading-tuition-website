We are not a one-size-fits-all service. Before any tuition begins, we take time to understand where your child is, where they need to be, and what is getting in the way.

GCSE and A-Level Support

At GCSE, students across Guildford's schools — including George Abbot School and Tormead School — are working towards qualifications that will determine their sixth-form options. Getting the right grades at this stage matters, and gaps in understanding that were manageable in Year 9 can become serious problems by the time mock exams arrive in Year 11.

Our GCSE tutors work closely with the specific syllabuses your child is following. Whether that is AQA English Language, OCR Gateway Science, or AQA Mathematics, we focus on the exact content, question styles, and mark scheme logic that examiners are looking for. We do not teach to a generic curriculum — we teach to your child's actual exam.

At A-Level, the step up in demand catches many able students off guard. Families in Guildford with children at Guildford High School, St Catherine's School, or the Royal Grammar School's sixth form often come to us when a student who sailed through GCSEs suddenly finds themselves struggling with the depth and independence required at A-Level. Our tutors help students rebuild confidence, close conceptual gaps, and develop the kind of analytical thinking that top universities expect to see.

For students targeting competitive universities — including those applying to Russell Group institutions or Oxbridge — we also support with personal statement preparation and subject-specific interview readiness.

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

The 11+ in Surrey is genuinely competitive. A place at the Royal Grammar School Guildford is sought after by families from across the county, and the standard required is high. The entrance process typically involves assessments in verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, mathematics, and English — and children who perform best are usually those who have had structured, consistent preparation well in advance of the exam.

Leading Tuition supports families through every stage of this process. Our approach includes:

  • An initial assessment to identify your child's starting point across all tested areas
  • A structured preparation plan built around the exam timeline
  • Regular practice with past papers and timed conditions
  • Targeted work on the specific question types that appear in Surrey 11+ assessments
  • Progress reviews so you always know how your child is developing

We are honest with families from the outset. Our goal is to give every child the best possible chance, and that means preparing them thoroughly — not just drilling them through papers without understanding. We also support families considering independent school entry at 11+, where schools such as Tormead and Guildford High have their own entrance assessments with different demands.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

Surrey has a significant number of families with children applying to medicine, dentistry, and other highly competitive degree programmes. Leading Tuition provides focused UCAT preparation, helping students understand the test's structure, develop time management strategies, and practise under realistic conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Guildford

When should we start preparing for the Royal Grammar School Guildford 11+ exam?

Most families begin structured preparation in Year 4 or early Year 5, giving around 12 to 18 months before the exam. Starting earlier allows time to build skills gradually rather than cramming, which tends to produce more confident and consistent performance on the day.

My child attends George Abbot School and is struggling with their OCR GCSE subjects — can you help?

Yes. Our tutors are familiar with OCR specifications and can work directly with the content and assessment style your child is encountering. We would begin with a short diagnostic session to identify where the difficulties lie before putting a plan in place.

Do you offer tuition for students applying to medicine from Guildford sixth forms?

We do. We support students with UCAT preparation, personal statement guidance, and medical school interview practice. We are up to date with current admissions requirements and can advise on how to approach the process strategically alongside A-Level study.

Can tuition take place in person if we live outside Guildford town centre, for example in Godalming or Shalford?

We offer both online and in-person tuition. Many families in the surrounding villages find that online sessions work extremely well — they remove travel time and allow for consistent scheduling. We are happy to discuss what would suit your family best when we speak.
