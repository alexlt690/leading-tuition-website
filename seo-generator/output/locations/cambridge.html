Private Tuition in Cambridge

Expert tutors supporting families across Cambridge

Book a Free Consultation

Cambridge is a city where academic ambition runs deep. Families here often hold high expectations for their children's education — and understandably so. Many students are aiming for Cambridge University itself, or other leading Russell Group institutions, and the culture of scholarship that permeates the city creates strong demand for private tuition across every year group, from Year 3 right through to sixth form. Whether your child attends a local independent school, a state secondary, or one of the city's well-regarded sixth form colleges, the pressure to perform is real — and the right tutor can make a significant difference.

Tutoring in Cambridge — What We Offer

Leading Tuition works with students across Cambridge and the surrounding area, including families in Newnham, Trumpington, Chesterton, and beyond. We match each student with a tutor who understands not just the subject, but the specific academic environment your child is working in. That means knowing the syllabuses taught locally, the expectations of Cambridge's most competitive schools, and the particular pressures that come with studying in a city where academic excellence is the norm rather than the exception.

Our tutors are experienced across a wide range of subjects and year groups. We support students with weekly subject tuition, targeted exam preparation, and longer-term academic development. Every tutoring arrangement begins with a conversation — we want to understand your child's needs, their school context, and what success looks like for your family.

GCSE and A-Level Support

At GCSE and A-Level, the right exam board knowledge is essential. In Cambridgeshire, OCR is the dominant board across many schools and subjects, and our tutors are thoroughly familiar with OCR specifications — including OCR A and OCR B variants in sciences, and OCR English Literature and History. We also support students following AQA and Edexcel courses, so wherever your child's school sits, we can help.

Students at The Perse School, The Stephen Perse Foundation, and Hills Road Sixth Form College often face particularly demanding internal assessments alongside public examinations. Hills Road and Long Road Sixth Form College both attract high-achieving students from across the region, and the step up to A-Level can be sharper than expected, even for students who sailed through GCSEs. Our tutors help bridge that gap — building genuine understanding rather than surface-level exam technique.

We support students across the full range of A-Level subjects, including Mathematics, Further Mathematics, Biology, Chemistry, Physics, English Literature, History, Economics, and Modern Languages. For GCSE students, we cover core subjects as well as options, and we're experienced in supporting students who need to consolidate foundations as well as those pushing for the highest grades.

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

Cambridge itself does not have grammar schools, so the 11+ is not a significant feature of the local landscape in the way it is in parts of Hertfordshire or Essex. However, Leading Tuition does support primary-age students and those in early secondary school who would benefit from structured academic support. This might mean building confidence in Maths or English at Key Stage 2, preparing for internal assessments at independent schools such as St Mary's School Cambridge or The Stephen Perse Foundation, or simply ensuring a smooth and well-supported transition into Year 7. If your family is considering selective independent schools, we can discuss what preparation makes sense for your child's specific situation.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

Cambridge has an unusually high proportion of students with Oxbridge in their sights — and Leading Tuition is well placed to support them. Gaining a place at Cambridge or Oxford requires more than strong predicted grades. Admissions tutors are looking for students who can think independently, engage critically with their subject, and perform under pressure in interview settings.

We offer support across the full admissions process, including:

  • Preparation for subject admissions tests, including the MAT (Mathematics), LNAT (Law), TSA (Thinking Skills Assessment), HAT (History), and ELAT (English Literature)
  • Interview preparation, including mock interviews with subject-specialist tutors
  • Personal statement guidance, helping students articulate genuine academic interest
  • UCAT preparation for students applying to medicine at Oxford, Cambridge, or Imperial
  • Subject-level deepening for students who need to go beyond the A-Level syllabus

For students applying to medicine, law, or other highly competitive courses, early preparation matters. We work with students from Year 12 onwards to build the academic confidence and critical thinking skills that admissions processes reward.

Why Cambridge Families Choose Leading Tuition

Parents in Cambridge are often highly informed about education — many work in research, academia, or professional fields, and they ask searching questions before trusting someone with their child's learning. We think that is entirely reasonable. Leading Tuition is built on the principle that tuition should be substantive, personalised, and genuinely effective — not a generic service dressed up with reassuring language.

Our tutors are subject specialists, not generalists covering every topic under the sun. We take time to understand each student's school context, their existing strengths, and the specific areas where they need support. Families in areas like Cherry Hinton and Ely who may not have easy access to in-person specialist support can access our online tuition, which follows exactly the same approach as face-to-face sessions.

We are transparent about how we work, honest about what tuition can and cannot achieve, and committed to keeping parents informed throughout. If something is not working, we say so — and we adjust.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Cambridge

My child attends Hills Road Sixth Form College and is struggling with the jump to A-Level. Can you help?

Yes — this is one of the most common situations we support. The transition to A-Level, even at a strong college like Hills Road, can be demanding. Our tutors are familiar with the depth of content required and can help your child build the understanding and independent study habits that A-Level success depends on.

My son or daughter is applying to Cambridge University. How early should we start admissions preparation?

Ideally, subject-level deepening should begin in Year 12, well before personal statements and admissions tests come into focus. Admissions tutors are looking for genuine intellectual engagement, and that takes time to develop. We recommend an initial conversation at the start of Year 12 so we can plan a realistic and unhurried approach.

Do you support students at independent schools like The Perse School or St Mary's School Cambridge?

We do. Students at selective independent schools often face high internal expectations alongside public exam preparation, and our tutors understand the academic culture of these schools. We work alongside what is being taught in school, not against it.

My child is in Year 5 and I want to make sure they are well prepared for secondary school. Is it too early to start?

Not at all. Early secondary preparation — building strong foundations in Maths and English, developing reading habits, and growing academic confidence — is something we support from upper Key Stage 2 onwards. Starting early means there is no last-minute pressure, and your child arrives at Year 7 feeling genuinely ready.
