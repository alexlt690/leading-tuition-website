GCSE and A-Level Support

Students at both Watford grammar schools are working within a high-attainment environment where expectations are significant. A-Level results at these schools are strong, but that also means the internal bar is high — students who were top of the year at primary find themselves in a cohort of equally able peers, and the transition can be demanding.

Hertfordshire schools follow predominantly AQA and OCR specifications. Our tutors are selected for their knowledge of these boards specifically — understanding the mark scheme structures and question styles that AQA and OCR use is not the same as knowing the content, and both matter. At GCSE, we support students across the sciences, mathematics, English, humanities, and languages. At A-Level, demand tends to concentrate in the subjects where the content step-up is steepest: mathematics, further mathematics, the sciences, and economics.

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

The 11+ for Watford Grammar School for Boys and Watford Grammar School for Girls is one of the most competitive in Hertfordshire. Both schools are heavily oversubscribed, with children applying from across the county and beyond. The assessment tests verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, English, and mathematics — and knowing the format in advance, and having practised under timed conditions, makes a real difference to how a child performs on the day.

St Michael's Catholic Grammar School in nearby Bishop's Stortford is a further option for Catholic families seeking a selective state place. For families considering independent schools, St Columba's College in St Albans sets its own entrance papers with a different character from the local authority tests.

When we work with a family on 11+ preparation, we begin with a thorough assessment of where the child is currently — not to label them, but to understand exactly what work needs to happen and in what order. From there, we build a structured programme that develops the skills being tested, builds speed under timed conditions, and ensures the child is ready when it matters.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Watford

How competitive is the 11+ for Watford Grammar School for Boys?

Very competitive. Both Watford grammar schools are significantly oversubscribed each year, with many hundreds of children competing for a limited number of places. Children who perform at the top of the assessment have typically been well prepared — they know the format, have practised under timed conditions, and have developed the verbal and non-verbal reasoning skills the test targets. Starting preparation in Year 4 gives a child the time to develop these skills properly.

My son is in Year 7 at Watford Grammar School for Boys and finding the transition harder than expected. Can you help?

This is very common. Grammar schools move quickly, and students who sailed through primary sometimes find the pace and expectation at grammar school a genuine shock. We help Year 7 students consolidate foundations in mathematics and English, build study habits, and develop the confidence to keep up with a demanding cohort.

We are in Rickmansworth — can you help with 11+ preparation for the Watford grammars?

Yes. We work with families across Watford, Rickmansworth, Chorleywood, Oxhey, and the surrounding area. Many sessions take place online, which is often the most practical option for busy families — but in-person is also available where preferred.

Does Leading Tuition support UCAT preparation for students applying to medical school?

Yes. UCAT preparation is one of our specialist areas. We support students with section-by-section strategy, timed practice, and performance analysis — and we help them build the speed and accuracy the test requires in the months leading up to their sitting in the summer before Year 13.
