Private Tuition in Bath

Expert tutors supporting families across Bath

Book a Free Consultation

Bath is a city with serious academic ambitions. The presence of the University of Bath, the proximity to Bristol, and a thriving independent school sector — including King Edward's School Bath, Prior Park College, and Kingswood School — mean that families across the city, from Larkhall to Oldfield Park, are thinking carefully about their children's education from an early age. Demand for private tuition is high, particularly around A-Level support, selective school preparation, and university applications. Whether your child is just finding their feet in secondary school or facing the pressure of sixth form exams, the academic stakes in Bath are real — and the support available needs to match them.

At Leading Tuition, we work with families across Bath and the surrounding area, including Bradford-on-Avon and Weston, providing expert one-to-one tuition tailored to each student's school, syllabus, and goals. Every student's journey is different, and the challenges they face shift at each stage. What we offer is consistent: experienced tutors, structured support, and a genuine understanding of what Bath students are up against.

Supporting Bath Students from 11+ to University

A student's academic journey in Bath rarely follows a straight line. The transition from primary to secondary school, the step up to GCSE, the demands of sixth form, and the complexity of university applications each bring their own pressures — and each requires a different kind of support.

For younger students, Leading Tuition offers primary-age and early secondary tuition focused on building strong foundations in core subjects. This isn't about drilling children through tests — it's about developing confidence, closing gaps before they widen, and ensuring students arrive at secondary school ready to thrive. For families in areas like Bathampton or Larkhall whose children are moving into Year 7 at schools such as Beechen Cliff School or Hayesfield Girls' School, that early preparation can make a meaningful difference to how settled and capable a student feels in those first crucial terms.

As students progress, the nature of support evolves. Our tutors adapt their approach to match the demands of each stage — whether that means consolidating understanding at GCSE, building independent thinking at A-Level, or preparing a personal statement for a competitive university course.

Preparing for Selective Schools and the 11+ in Bath

Bath does not have grammar schools, so the 11+ examination is not a feature of the local state school system. However, a number of Bath's independent schools — including King Edward's School Bath and Prior Park College — have their own entrance assessments, and competition for places is genuine. These assessments vary by school and typically test reasoning, English, and mathematics at a level that rewards preparation.

Leading Tuition supports families navigating independent school entry, helping students build the skills and confidence needed to perform well in these assessments. We also work with students at the early secondary stage who may be considering a school move at 13+ or 16+, ensuring they are well-prepared for whatever entrance process lies ahead. The goal is always the same: a student who walks into an assessment feeling ready, not anxious.

Raising Grades at GCSE — Bath Schools and Exam Boards

GCSE is where academic pressure becomes most visible for many Bath families. The dominant exam board across Bath's state and independent schools is OCR, though students at some schools will also encounter AQA and Edexcel specifications depending on the subject. Our tutors are familiar with all three and work directly from the relevant syllabus — not a generic curriculum.

Common subjects where students seek support include:

  • Mathematics and Further Mathematics
  • English Language and English Literature
  • Biology, Chemistry, and Physics (separate sciences)
  • History, Geography, and Modern Languages
  • Computer Science and Economics

Students at Beechen Cliff School, Hayesfield Girls' School, Kingswood School, and Prior Park College all follow specifications that our tutors know well. Whether a student needs to address a specific weak area or wants to push from a grade 6 to a 7 or 8, we build a plan around their actual papers, mark schemes, and assessment objectives — not a one-size-fits-all programme.

A-Level Tuition and Sixth Form Support in Bath

A-Level is where the gap between classroom teaching and individual understanding becomes most consequential. Class sizes, pace, and the sheer volume of content mean that even capable students can fall behind without realising it — and by the time mock results arrive, there may be less time than families expect to turn things around.

Leading Tuition works with sixth form students across Bath's independent and state schools, supporting OCR A-Level courses in particular, alongside AQA and Edexcel where relevant. Our tutors are subject specialists — not generalists — and many have experience teaching or examining at A-Level themselves. For students at King Edward's School Bath or Prior Park College aiming for competitive university courses, that depth of subject knowledge matters.

We also support students who are retaking modules or sitting exams as private candidates, and we work with families in Bradford-on-Avon and Weston who need tutors willing to travel or work online.

Medical School and Oxbridge Preparation for Bath Applicants

Each year, a number of Bath students apply to Oxford, Cambridge, or medical school — and the preparation required goes well beyond strong A-Level grades. Personal statements, admissions tests, and interviews each demand a specific kind of readiness that standard school provision rarely covers in full.

Leading Tuition supports Bath applicants with UCAT preparation for medical school entry, subject-specific Oxbridge interview coaching, and personal statement guidance. We work with students to develop the kind of analytical thinking and intellectual confidence that admissions tutors are looking for — not rehearsed answers, but genuine depth of understanding. For students at King Edward's School Bath or Kingswood School with Oxbridge ambitions, starting this preparation early in Year 12 gives the best possible foundation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Leading Tuition cover my child's specific school and exam board?

Yes. Before any tuition begins, we establish exactly which school your child attends, which exam board and specification they are following, and what their current targets are. In Bath, this most commonly means OCR specifications at GCSE and A-Level, but our tutors are equally experienced with AQA and Edexcel. We work from your child's actual syllabus, past papers, and mark schemes — not a generic programme.

My child is moving from GCSE to A-Level. Can Leading Tuition help with that transition?

This is one of the most common points at which families come to us. The step up from GCSE to A-Level is significant — the volume of content increases, the style of questioning changes, and students are expected to work with much greater independence. We help students build the study habits, subject understanding, and exam technique they need to make that transition successfully, ideally starting in the summer before Year 12 begins.

My child's exams are only a few weeks away. Is it too late to start tuition?

It is rarely too late to make a difference. We offer intensive revision programmes designed for students who need focused, efficient support in a short timeframe. These sessions are structured around the highest-priority topics, past paper practice, and targeted feedback — making the most of the time available. We are honest with families about what is realistic, and we focus on what will have the greatest impact before the exam date.

What happens if my child's grades do not improve?

We take this question seriously. From the outset, we track progress carefully and communicate openly with parents about how sessions are going. If a student is not making the progress we would expect, we review our approach — adjusting the focus, the pace, or the style of support — rather than continuing with something that is not working. Our tutors are experienced enough to identify when a different strategy is needed, and we would always rather have an honest conversation early than wait until results day.
