Wimbledon has one of the strongest concentrations of independent school excellence in South West London. King's College School Wimbledon is one of the most academically selective independent schools in the UK — its A-Level results and Oxbridge success rate are consistently among the highest in the country — and Wimbledon High School (GDST) is the leading independent girls' school in the area. Families in Raynes Park, Merton Park, and the wider SW19 area are navigating high expectations and competitive entry at every stage. At Leading Tuition, we support Wimbledon families with precisely the kind of expert, targeted support these schools and their students require.

GCSE and A-Level Support

Students at King's College School Wimbledon are working within one of the most demanding academic environments in the UK. The pace is fast, the cohort is exceptionally able, and the expectation — both internal and from parents — is that students will perform at the very top of the grade boundaries. For students aiming at Oxbridge or medicine, A*s across the board are the expectation, not the aspiration.

At Wimbledon High School, similar expectations apply, and students who are finding specific subjects difficult benefit enormously from one-to-one support that is tailored to their exact course. Schools like these follow predominantly AQA and Edexcel specifications. Our tutors are selected for their knowledge of these boards and their ability to target exactly the areas where a student is losing marks.

At GCSE and A-Level, we support students across all subjects, with particular strength in mathematics, further mathematics, the sciences, economics, and English — the subjects where demand from Wimbledon families is most consistent.

11+ and Independent School Preparation

Entry to King's College School Wimbledon at 11+ is fiercely competitive. The school sets its own entrance papers in English and Mathematics, and the standard required is high. Candidates also sit a cognitive abilities assessment. Wimbledon High School uses a similar approach — its own English and Maths papers alongside a reasoning element.

The London Borough of Merton has no grammar schools, so the 11+ for state schools is not relevant here. For families focused on independent school entry, preparation needs to be calibrated to the specific school's papers — not to a local authority 11+ format. We work with children from Year 3 upwards, building the mathematical fluency, reading comprehension, and written expression skills that underpin performance in these assessments.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

King's College School and Wimbledon High School send significant numbers of students to Oxbridge each year. Leading Tuition supports these students with Oxbridge admissions test preparation (MAT, LNAT, TSA, BMAT history, and others), personal statement development, and interview coaching. We also support students applying to medicine with UCAT preparation and MMI coaching. For students at these schools, admissions preparation is an extension of academic excellence — not a bolt-on — and that is how we approach it.

Why Wimbledon Families Choose Leading Tuition

Families in Wimbledon expect their child's tutor to be at a comparable academic level to the school itself. Our tutors are Oxford and Cambridge graduates who have been through the same demanding selection processes their students are preparing for. We understand King's College School's culture and expectations, we know what Wimbledon High School looks for in its students, and we bring that specific knowledge to every session.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Wimbledon

How competitive is entry to King's College School Wimbledon at 11+?

Very competitive. KCS is one of the most selective independent schools in South London, with its own entrance papers and a small number of places. The children who gain entry have typically been specifically prepared for the school's assessments — not just for a generic 11+ format — and have strong foundations in both Mathematics and English. Starting preparation in Year 3 or 4 is advisable for families seriously targeting this school.

My son is at KCS and working towards Oxbridge entry — can you help with MAT preparation?

Yes. MAT (the Mathematics Admissions Test for Oxford entry to Mathematics and related courses) is one of the specialist areas our tutors cover. We work with students on the problem-solving techniques, mathematical reasoning, and specific content areas the MAT targets, going significantly beyond A-Level content.

We are in Raynes Park — do you cover our area?

Yes. We work with families across Wimbledon, Raynes Park, Merton Park, Colliers Wood, South Wimbledon, and Mitcham. Most sessions are conducted online, but in-person is available where preferred.

Can you help with preparation for Wimbledon High School's 11+ entrance?

Yes. Wimbledon High School's entrance process includes the school's own papers in English and Mathematics. We work with children from Year 3 upwards to build the specific skills these papers require — not generic 11+ preparation, but preparation calibrated to the standard and format that Wimbledon High sets.
