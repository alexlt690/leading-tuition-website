Tutoring in Leicester — What We Offer

Leading Tuition provides one-to-one private tuition for students from primary age through to A-Level and beyond. Our tutors are experienced in the curricula and exam formats that Leicester students actually encounter — including the AQA and OCR specifications that dominate GCSE and A-Level teaching across Leicestershire's schools. Whether your child attends a state secondary, an independent school, or is preparing for a significant academic transition, we match them with a tutor who understands exactly what is expected of them.

We work with students at every stage: building confidence in primary school, consolidating key skills at Key Stage 3, preparing for high-stakes GCSE examinations, and supporting the more demanding independent study required at A-Level. Sessions are focused, purposeful, and built around your child — not a generic lesson plan.

GCSE and A-Level Support

The majority of secondary schools in Leicester and the surrounding area — including Gartree School in Oadby — follow AQA and OCR specifications across core subjects. This matters, because exam technique, mark scheme language, and assessment structure vary significantly between boards. A tutor who knows the specific demands of AQA English Language or OCR A-Level Chemistry is far better placed to help your child than one working from general subject knowledge alone.

At GCSE, we support students across all core and option subjects, with particular demand for Maths, the Sciences, English, and Modern Foreign Languages. At A-Level, we work with students preparing for university entry across a wide range of subjects, helping them develop the depth of understanding and independent analytical skills that top universities expect to see.

Students at schools such as Leicester Grammar School and Loughborough Grammar School often face a particularly high bar — both internally and in terms of where they are aiming next. Our tutors are well-versed in supporting students at academically selective schools, where the pace is faster and the expectations are higher.

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

Unlike some parts of England, Leicestershire does not operate a selective grammar school system, so there is no local 11+ examination in the traditional sense. However, families in areas like Stoneygate and Oadby who are considering independent schools — including Stoneygate School and Leicester Grammar School — may encounter their own admissions assessments, and early academic preparation remains genuinely valuable.

Leading Tuition supports primary-age children and early secondary students in building strong foundations in literacy, numeracy, and reasoning. This kind of structured support at Year 4, 5, or 6 pays dividends not just in any school entry process, but in how confidently a child approaches secondary school from day one. We focus on developing real understanding rather than drilling for tests — because children who genuinely understand the material perform better under any assessment conditions.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

The University of Leicester is home to one of the UK's well-regarded medical schools, and it attracts applicants from across the country. For Leicester-based students, there is both the advantage of familiarity and the pressure of knowing how competitive local and national applications have become. Achieving the grades is only part of the challenge — medical applicants also need to perform well in the UCAT, demonstrate meaningful work experience, and present a compelling personal statement.

Leading Tuition works with A-Level students preparing for medicine and other competitive degree programmes, including those targeting Russell Group universities. Our support covers:

  • A-Level subject tutoring in Biology, Chemistry, and Mathematics at the depth required for competitive university entry
  • UCAT preparation, including practice across all five subtests and timed technique work
  • Personal statement guidance, helping students articulate their motivations clearly and authentically
  • Interview preparation for medical school and other competitive courses

For students applying to Oxford or Cambridge, we also provide subject-specific support and help with admissions tests relevant to their chosen course.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Leicester

My child attends Leicester Grammar School and is struggling to keep up with the pace — can you help?

Yes. We regularly support students at academically selective independent schools where the curriculum moves quickly and the internal expectations are high. We work within the specific subject content and exam board specifications your child's school uses, so sessions complement rather than conflict with their school learning.

Is there any benefit to tuition if my child is in Year 5 or 6 and we are not sitting an 11+ exam?

Absolutely. Strong foundations in literacy and numeracy at primary age make a significant difference to how confidently children transition into secondary school. We focus on genuine understanding rather than test preparation, which serves children well regardless of the school they move into.

My son wants to apply to Leicester Medical School — when should we start preparing for the UCAT?

Most students begin focused UCAT preparation in the spring or early summer of Year 12, giving them time to sit the test in the summer before their UCAS application. Starting A-Level subject support earlier — particularly in Biology and Chemistry — ensures the academic foundation is strong well before the admissions process begins.

Do your tutors know the AQA and OCR specifications, or do they teach general subject knowledge?

Our tutors work specifically to the exam board and specification your child is studying. For GCSE and A-Level students in Leicester, that typically means AQA or OCR, and our tutors are familiar with the mark schemes, command words, and assessment objectives that determine how marks are actually awarded.
