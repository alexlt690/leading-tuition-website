Leicester is a city with real academic ambition. Families across Oadby, Stoneygate, and the wider city are increasingly seeking private tuition to give their children a meaningful edge — whether that means preparing for entry to selective independent schools, building confidence ahead of GCSEs, or working towards a place at a competitive university. There is particular demand among families with an eye on the University of Leicester's medical school, as well as Russell Group destinations further afield. Leading Tuition works with students at every stage of this journey, providing structured, expert support that is tailored to the specific pressures Leicester families face.

Tutoring in Leicester — What We Offer

Leading Tuition provides one-to-one tuition for students from primary age through to sixth form and beyond. Our tutors are experienced, subject-specialist professionals who understand the curriculum demands placed on students in Leicester's schools — from the independent sector to the city's strong state secondaries. Sessions are available online, giving families in Knighton, Evington, Wigston, and across the city flexible access to high-quality support without the need to travel.

We work closely with each family to understand what their child actually needs — not just the subject, but the gaps in understanding, the confidence issues, and the specific exam requirements they are working towards. Tuition is planned carefully and reviewed regularly, so progress is always purposeful.

GCSE and A-Level Support

The majority of Leicester's secondary schools follow AQA and OCR specifications, and our tutors are thoroughly familiar with both. Whether a student is working through AQA GCSE English Language, OCR A-Level Biology, or any of the core sciences and humanities at either level, we match them with a tutor who knows the mark schemes, the common pitfalls, and the most effective ways to prepare.

Students at schools such as Gartree School and Leicester Grammar School often face different pressures — one navigating the demands of a high-performing state school, the other managing the pace of an academically selective independent environment. Our tutors understand both contexts and adapt accordingly. For A-Level students, we place particular emphasis on exam technique alongside subject knowledge, since it is often the ability to communicate understanding clearly under pressure that determines the final grade.

We support students across a wide range of subjects at GCSE and A-Level, including:

  • Mathematics and Further Mathematics
  • Biology, Chemistry, and Physics
  • English Language and English Literature
  • History, Geography, and Economics
  • French, Spanish, and other modern languages

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

Leicester itself does not operate a grammar school system, so the high-stakes 11+ preparation that dominates tuition demand in some other parts of the country is less of a feature here. That said, a number of families in areas such as Oadby and Stoneygate do consider selective independent schools — including Leicester Grammar School and Stoneygate School — and entrance to these schools involves its own assessment process that benefits from careful preparation.

More broadly, Leading Tuition works with primary-age students and those in early secondary to build strong foundations in literacy, numeracy, and reasoning. This kind of early support is not about drilling for a single test — it is about developing the skills and habits that make a child a more capable, confident learner throughout their school career. For families considering Loughborough Grammar School, which draws students from across Leicestershire, we can also provide targeted preparation for their admissions process.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

The University of Leicester has one of the UK's well-regarded medical schools, and it attracts significant local interest from sixth-form students across the city. Gaining a place requires more than strong A-Level grades — applicants need a competitive UCAT score, a well-constructed personal statement, and the ability to perform under pressure in an interview setting.

Leading Tuition supports aspiring medical students with UCAT preparation, personal statement guidance, and interview coaching. We also work with students applying to other competitive programmes — law, dentistry, veterinary science, and Russell Group courses across a range of disciplines. Our university admissions support is grounded in an honest understanding of what selectors are looking for, and we help students present themselves with clarity and genuine conviction rather than rehearsed answers.

Why Leicester Families Choose Leading Tuition

Parents in Leicester tell us that what they value most is consistency and communication. They want a tutor who turns up prepared, who genuinely knows their child's syllabus, and who keeps them informed about progress. That is what we aim to provide with every student we work with.

Families from Wigston to Stoneygate, and from Evington to the city centre, trust Leading Tuition because we take the time to get the match right — the right tutor, the right approach, and the right level of challenge for each individual student.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Leicester

My child attends Leicester Grammar School and is struggling to keep up with the pace — can Leading Tuition help?

Yes. We regularly support students at academically selective independent schools where the pace and depth of teaching can be demanding. We work to identify exactly where the gaps are and help students consolidate their understanding so they can engage more confidently in class.

Are your tutors familiar with the AQA and OCR specifications used in Leicester's schools?

They are. We match students with tutors who have direct experience of the relevant specification — not just the subject in general. This matters particularly at GCSE and A-Level, where mark scheme awareness and exam technique are closely tied to the specific board being followed.

We live in Oadby — do you offer in-person tuition or is everything online?

All of our tuition is delivered online, which works well for families across Oadby and the wider Leicester area. Sessions take place via a professional virtual classroom with interactive tools, and most families find the flexibility of online tuition a significant practical advantage.

My daughter is applying to the University of Leicester medical school next year — when should she start preparing for the UCAT?

Ideally, UCAT preparation should begin in the spring or early summer of Year 12, allowing enough time to build familiarity with each subtest before the testing window opens in July. Starting early also means there is time to identify weaker areas and address them properly, rather than cramming in the final weeks.
