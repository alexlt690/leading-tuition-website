Aston University Medicine Entry Requirements

Aston University is one of the most exciting new additions to UK medical education. Opening its doors to medical students in 2025, Aston's medical school is built around a clear purpose: training doctors who are ready to serve diverse, urban communities. Located in the heart of Birmingham — one of the most clinically rich and demographically varied cities in the country — Aston offers something genuinely different from the established medical schools. If you are drawn to a city-based education, want early and meaningful clinical exposure, and are looking for a programme that has been designed from the ground up with modern medical practice in mind, Aston University deserves serious consideration.

Why Choose Aston University for Medicine?

Aston University has a long-standing reputation for producing graduates who go directly into industry and professional practice. Its pharmacy and optometry programmes are well-regarded, and the new medical school builds on that same applied, career-focused ethos. Rather than simply replicating what older medical schools do, Aston has had the opportunity to design a curriculum that reflects how medicine is actually practised today — with an emphasis on integrated learning, patient contact from early in the course, and a strong awareness of health inequalities.

Birmingham itself is a significant draw. It is the youngest major city in Europe by population, with extraordinary ethnic and socioeconomic diversity. For a medical student, this translates into clinical placements that expose you to a genuinely wide range of conditions, presentations, and patient backgrounds. The city is home to major NHS trusts including University Hospitals Birmingham NHS Foundation Trust, which runs the Queen Elizabeth Hospital — one of the largest acute hospitals in the country. Training in this environment gives you a breadth of experience that is difficult to replicate in smaller or more homogeneous cities.

Aston's campus is compact and urban, sitting close to Birmingham city centre. Students benefit from excellent transport links, a lively student community, and the cultural and social richness of a major UK city — without the cost of living pressures associated with London.

Entry Requirements and A-Level Grades

Aston University's typical A-Level offer for Medicine is AAA, with Chemistry required as one of the three subjects. Biology is also expected, either as a second A-Level or demonstrated through equivalent scientific study. Mathematics and Physics are welcomed but not mandatory. It is worth checking Aston's admissions pages directly as requirements for a new programme may be refined ahead of each admissions cycle.

As a new medical school, Aston is likely to be competitive from the outset. Do not assume that a lower offer means lower competition — applicants who meet the academic threshold will be assessed holistically, and the quality of your wider application will matter significantly. Predicted grades will be considered carefully, and any downward trend in academic performance is likely to be scrutinised.

Aston welcomes applications from students taking Scottish Highers, the International Baccalaureate, and other equivalent qualifications. If you are studying a non-standard curriculum, contact the admissions team early to confirm how your qualifications will be assessed.

UCAT Requirements at Aston University

Aston University uses the UCAT (University Clinical Aptitude Test) as part of its admissions process. As a new medical school opening in 2025, Aston has not yet published a long track record of score thresholds, but applicants should aim for a strong performance across all five subtests: Verbal Reasoning, Decision Making, Quantitative Reasoning, Abstract Reasoning, and Situational Judgement.

In practice, competitive applicants to UK medical schools typically aim for a total score in the region of 2700 or above, with a Situational Judgement score of Band 1 or Band 2. Given that Aston is a new school attracting significant interest, it would be unwise to assume a lower bar. Prepare for the UCAT thoroughly and treat it as a serious component of your application — not an afterthought once your A-Level preparation is underway.

The Interview Process at Aston University

Aston University uses a Multiple Mini Interview (MMI) format. This means that rather than a single panel interview, you will move through a series of short, structured stations — typically between six and ten — each lasting around five to eight minutes. Each station is assessed independently by a different interviewer, and the topics rotate across different competency areas.

At a typical MMI, you might encounter stations covering ethical dilemmas, role-play scenarios with an actor, questions about your motivation for medicine, communication tasks, or discussions of current healthcare issues. The format is designed to give a more rounded picture of a candidate than a single interview can provide, and it reduces the impact of nerves at any one moment.

Preparation for an MMI should focus on practising out loud — not memorising scripts, but developing the habit of structuring your thinking clearly under time pressure. Understanding NHS values, being able to discuss medical ethics frameworks, and having reflected carefully on your work experience are all important foundations.

What Makes a Strong Aston University Application

Beyond strong grades and a competitive UCAT score, Aston will be looking for applicants who can demonstrate genuine engagement with medicine and healthcare. Key qualities include:

  • Relevant work experience — shadowing a GP, volunteering in a care setting, or any role that has brought you into contact with patients and healthcare professionals
  • Insight into the NHS — an understanding of how healthcare is delivered in the UK, including current pressures and priorities
  • Commitment to serving diverse communities — given Aston's urban Birmingham setting, an awareness of health inequalities is particularly relevant
  • Communication and teamwork — demonstrated through extracurricular activities, leadership roles, or positions of responsibility
  • Reflective thinking — the ability to learn from experience and articulate what medicine means to you personally

Your personal statement should be specific and reflective. Avoid listing activities without explaining what you took from them. Admissions tutors read thousands of statements — what distinguishes a strong one is honest, thoughtful reflection rather than an impressive-sounding list.

Aston is expected to offer approximately 100 places per year in its initial cohorts, making it a smaller intake than many established medical schools. This means the admissions process will be selective, and every element of your application needs to be considered carefully.

Frequently Asked Questions about Applying to Aston University

What UCAT score do I need for Aston University Medicine?

Aston has not yet published a fixed UCAT cut-off, but as a competitive new medical school you should aim for a total score of around 2700 or higher, with a Situational Judgement score of Band 1 or Band 2. Sitting the UCAT early in the summer gives you time to resit if needed and ensures your score is available when applications open.

Is work experience required to apply to Aston University Medicine?

Work experience is not formally required, but it is strongly expected. Admissions tutors want to see that you have engaged with healthcare in a meaningful way and can reflect on what you observed. Even a modest amount of well-reflected experience is more valuable than a long list of activities you cannot speak to in depth.

How should I prepare for the Aston University MMI?

The most effective preparation involves practising MMI-style stations out loud, ideally with a partner or tutor who can give feedback. Familiarise yourself with common ethical frameworks, NHS values, and current healthcare topics. Reflect carefully on your work experience so you can draw on it naturally during role-play or motivation stations. Timed practice is essential — the short station format rewards candidates who can think and communicate quickly.

Does Aston University accept international students for Medicine?

As a new medical school, Aston's policy on international applicants is still being confirmed. UK home students are the primary focus for initial cohorts, as is common with new medical programmes. If you are an international student, contact Aston's admissions office directly to confirm your eligibility before investing time in an application.
