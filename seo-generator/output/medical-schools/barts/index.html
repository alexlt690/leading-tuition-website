Why Choose Barts and The London School of Medicine for Medicine?

Barts and The London School of Medicine and Dentistry, part of Queen Mary University of London, occupies a genuinely unique position in UK medical education. It is one of the oldest medical institutions in the country — Barts Hospital itself dates back to 1123 — yet the school is thoroughly modern in how it trains doctors. What draws students here is rarely just prestige. It is the combination of a research-intensive, problem-based learning curriculum, clinical exposure from the very first year, and a location in east London that places you at the heart of one of the most medically and demographically diverse patient populations anywhere in the world. If you want to train in an environment where the full complexity of medicine is visible from day one, Barts and The London is a serious contender. It is also, by any measure, one of the most competitive medical schools in the country — so if you are considering applying, it is worth understanding exactly what the school is looking for.

Entry Requirements and A-Level Grades

The standard A-Level offer at Barts and The London is A*AA, with Chemistry required as one of your three subjects. Biology is strongly recommended and, in practice, the vast majority of successful applicants hold both Chemistry and Biology at A-Level. The A* can be in any of the three subjects, though many applicants achieve it in a science. If you are sitting Scottish Highers or the International Baccalaureate, equivalent grades are considered — typically 38 points or above in the IB with strong scores at Higher Level in relevant sciences.

The school does not accept General Studies or Critical Thinking as part of your offer. If you are a graduate applicant, Barts and The London does consider applications from those with a relevant degree, though the standard undergraduate route remains the primary pathway. There are approximately 280 to 300 places available each year across the five-year MBBS programme, making competition for each place significant.

It is also worth noting that Barts and The London has a contextual admissions process. Applicants from under-represented backgrounds or lower-performing schools may be considered in context, though this does not lower the academic bar substantially — it simply ensures the school is reading your application fairly.

UCAT Requirements at Barts and The London School of Medicine

Barts and The London uses the UCAT as a core part of its selection process and is widely regarded as one of the most competitive London medical schools when it comes to UCAT thresholds. The school does not publish a fixed cut-off score, but in practice, applicants who are shortlisted for interview tend to have a total UCAT score well above the national average — typically in the range of 2700 or above across the four cognitive subtests, with a strong Situational Judgement score (Band 1 or Band 2 is expected).

The UCAT is used alongside your academic profile to decide who is invited to interview. A very high UCAT score can strengthen an application where predicted grades are at the lower end of the competitive range, and a weaker UCAT score will make it significantly harder to progress even with excellent grades. If you are targeting Barts and The London, investing serious preparation time in the UCAT — ideally beginning in Year 12 — is not optional.

The Interview Process at Barts and The London School of Medicine

Barts and The London uses a Multiple Mini Interview (MMI) format. This means that rather than a single panel interview, you rotate through a series of short, structured stations — typically around eight to ten — each lasting a few minutes. Each station is assessed independently by a different interviewer, and the topics vary considerably between stations.

You might encounter a station asking you to discuss an ethical dilemma in healthcare, another where you are asked to reflect on a work experience encounter, a communication or role-play scenario, a station testing your understanding of the NHS or current medical issues, and stations that probe your motivation for medicine and your personal qualities. The MMI format is designed to give a broader picture of who you are than a single interview can provide — and it rewards candidates who are genuinely reflective, can think on their feet, and communicate clearly under mild pressure. Rehearsing with a partner or tutor across a range of station types is the most effective preparation.

What Makes a Strong Barts and The London School of Medicine Application

Beyond grades and UCAT scores, Barts and The London is looking for applicants who demonstrate genuine insight into what medicine involves — and who can show they have sought that understanding out, not just read about it. Work experience is not formally mandated, but in practice, competitive applicants will have spent meaningful time in healthcare settings, whether that is hospital shadowing, care work, volunteering, or a combination. What matters is what you took from it.

Strong applicants to Barts and The London tend to share several characteristics:

  • Consistent academic performance with evidence of intellectual curiosity beyond the syllabus
  • Work experience that has been reflected on thoughtfully, not just listed
  • An understanding of the NHS, including current pressures and challenges
  • Awareness of health inequalities — particularly relevant given the school's east London setting
  • Evidence of resilience, teamwork, and communication through extracurricular activities or personal circumstances

Your personal statement should not simply recount what you have done — it should show how your experiences have shaped your understanding of medicine as a career. Barts and The London values students who are self-aware and who have thought seriously about the realities of clinical practice.

Frequently Asked Questions about Applying to Barts and The London School of Medicine

What UCAT score do I need for Barts and The London?

The school does not publish a fixed minimum, but competitive applicants typically score around 2700 or higher across the four cognitive subtests, with a Situational Judgement score of Band 1 or Band 2. Given how competitive the London medical school pool is, aiming for the top third of UCAT scores nationally is a sensible target if Barts and The London is a priority choice.

Is work experience required to apply?

There is no formal requirement, but it is strongly expected. Admissions tutors want to see that you understand what medicine involves in practice. A range of experiences — clinical and non-clinical — is more valuable than a single placement. Reflecting meaningfully on what you observed and learned is what distinguishes strong personal statements.

How should I prepare for the MMI at Barts and The London?

The most effective preparation involves practising across a wide variety of station types — ethics, communication, reflection, current NHS issues, and motivation. Timed practice with a partner or tutor is far more useful than reading alone. Familiarise yourself with common medical ethics frameworks and stay informed about healthcare news in the months before your interview.

Does Barts and The London accept international students?

Yes, Barts and The London does accept international and EU students, though the number of places available to overseas applicants is limited and competition is extremely high. International applicants must meet the same academic and UCAT requirements as home applicants and should check the latest fee and visa guidance on the Queen Mary University of London website before applying.
