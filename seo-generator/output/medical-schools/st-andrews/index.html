University of St Andrews Medicine Entry Requirements

St Andrews is one of the most distinctive medical schools in the UK — not despite its unusual structure, but because of it. The university offers a pre-clinical BSc (Hons) in Medicine, meaning you spend your first three years in the historic town of St Andrews before transferring to a partner medical school for your clinical training. It is a path that suits students who want a rigorous, research-informed grounding in the biomedical sciences within one of the UK's oldest and most academically serious universities. If you are drawn to a close-knit academic community, a genuinely collegiate atmosphere, and a degree that takes the science of medicine seriously before you ever set foot on a ward, St Andrews deserves careful consideration.

Why Choose University of St Andrews for Medicine?

St Andrews consistently ranks among the top universities in the UK for student satisfaction and academic quality, and its medical school reflects that broader culture. The pre-clinical programme is taught in small cohorts — around 160 students per year — which means you are not lost in a lecture theatre of hundreds. Tutorials are genuinely small, contact with academic staff is real, and the learning environment is one where curiosity is expected rather than optional.

The BSc (Hons) structure is worth understanding clearly. After three years in St Andrews, graduates transfer to one of several partner medical schools — including the University of Manchester, University of Edinburgh, and others — to complete their clinical MB ChB. This is not a disadvantage. Many students find that arriving at clinical school with a strong scientific foundation and an honours degree gives them a meaningful head start. The transition is well-supported, and St Andrews has long-established relationships with its partner institutions.

The town itself is part of the appeal. St Andrews is small, safe, and genuinely beautiful — a coastal university town with a strong sense of identity. Student life is unusually immersive because the university dominates the town, and the medical cohort is tight-knit. For students who want to feel part of a real academic community rather than a large urban campus, this environment is hard to replicate.

Entry Requirements and A-Level Grades

The standard A-Level offer for Medicine at St Andrews is AAA, with Chemistry required as one of the three subjects. Biology is strongly recommended and, in practice, almost all successful applicants offer it. Mathematics or Physics can strengthen an application, particularly for students interested in the quantitative side of biomedical science.

Scottish Highers applicants are typically considered on AAAAB or AAAAA at Higher, with Advanced Highers also taken into account. For students taking the International Baccalaureate, the typical requirement is around 36 points overall, with 6,6,5 at Higher Level including Chemistry.

St Andrews does not make A*AA offers as a standard condition, which distinguishes it from some other leading medical schools. However, this should not be read as a lower bar overall — the UCAT, interview, and personal statement all carry significant weight, and the competition for approximately 160 places each year is strong.

UCAT Requirements at University of St Andrews

St Andrews uses the UCAT as part of its admissions process, and your score will influence whether you are invited to interview. The university does not publish a fixed cut-off score, but in practice, competitive applicants tend to score in the upper half of the national cohort — a total score above 2600 across the four cognitive subtests is generally considered competitive, though this varies year on year depending on the applicant pool.

The Situational Judgement Test (SJT) is also considered. A Band 4 result is unlikely to support a strong application, and most successful candidates achieve Band 1 or Band 2. If your UCAT score is lower than you hoped, it is worth being realistic about how it may affect your chances here, and whether additional preparation before a resit or reapplication would be worthwhile.

Because St Andrews offers a BSc (Hons) rather than a full MB ChB, the admissions process is specifically selecting students for a pre-clinical science degree. The UCAT is used as one measure of academic and reasoning aptitude alongside your predicted grades and the rest of your application.

The Interview Process at University of St Andrews

St Andrews uses a traditional panel interview format. Shortlisted applicants are invited to attend an interview with a panel typically comprising academic staff and, in some cases, a clinician or current student. Interviews are usually held in person in St Andrews, though the university has offered online alternatives in recent years.

The panel format means you will face a sustained conversation rather than a series of timed stations. Interviewers are likely to explore your motivation for medicine, your understanding of the pre-clinical and clinical structure of the degree, your scientific curiosity, and your awareness of current issues in healthcare. Expect to be challenged on your personal statement — anything you have written about is fair game, and interviewers will probe whether your interest in a topic is genuine or surface-level.

Ethical scenarios and questions about NHS challenges are common. You do not need clinical expertise, but you should be able to think clearly under pressure and articulate your reasoning with confidence.

What Makes a Strong University of St Andrews Application

Successful applicants to St Andrews tend to share a few qualities beyond strong predicted grades:

  • Genuine academic curiosity — the pre-clinical programme is science-heavy, and students who thrive here tend to find biomedical science intrinsically interesting, not just a means to an end
  • Relevant work experience — shadowing, volunteering in healthcare settings, or care work all demonstrate commitment and give you material to reflect on in your personal statement and interview
  • A well-constructed personal statement — St Andrews values reflection over a list of activities; explain what you learned, not just what you did
  • Comfort with the BSc structure — applicants who understand and can articulate why the pre-clinical model appeals to them stand out from those who seem unaware of how the degree works
  • Resilience and independence — the transfer to a partner school after three years requires adaptability, and interviewers are likely to probe how you handle change

Frequently Asked Questions about Applying to University of St Andrews

What UCAT score do I need for St Andrews?
St Andrews does not publish a fixed threshold, but competitive applicants typically score above 2600 across the four cognitive subtests, with a Situational Judgement result of Band 1 or Band 2. Scores are considered alongside the rest of your application rather than used as a standalone filter.

Is work experience required to apply?
Work experience is not formally required, but it is strongly advisable. St Andrews expects applicants to demonstrate genuine insight into medicine and patient care. Shadowing a GP, volunteering in a care home, or working in any healthcare-adjacent role gives you the material to write a reflective personal statement and to answer interview questions with credibility.

How should I prepare for the panel interview?
Start by knowing your personal statement thoroughly — every claim you make should be something you can discuss in depth. Read broadly about NHS issues, medical ethics, and current healthcare challenges. Practise speaking clearly under pressure, ideally with someone who will ask follow-up questions rather than just listen. Mock interviews with a tutor or teacher who can give honest feedback are particularly useful for the panel format.

Does St Andrews accept international students for Medicine?
Yes, St Andrews does accept international students, including those from outside the UK and EU. International applicants are subject to the same academic and UCAT requirements, though tuition fees differ significantly from those for home students. The university's international office can provide up-to-date guidance on visa requirements and fee structures, and it is worth checking the admissions pages directly for the most current information each cycle.
