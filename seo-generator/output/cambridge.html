Cambridge is a city where academic ambition runs deep. Families here often hold high expectations for their children's education — and understandably so. With Cambridge University on the doorstep, many students grow up with Oxbridge as a genuine goal rather than a distant aspiration. That culture of scholarship creates strong demand for private tuition across all year groups, from Year 3 right through to sixth form and beyond. Whether your child attends a local independent school, a state secondary, or one of the city's outstanding sixth form colleges, Leading Tuition works with Cambridge families to help students reach their full potential.

Tutoring in Cambridge — What We Offer

Leading Tuition provides one-to-one tutoring for students at every stage of their education, from primary through to A-Level and university admissions. Our tutors are subject specialists who understand the specific demands of the Cambridge academic environment — including the schools, the exam boards, and the competitive landscape that local families navigate every year.

We work with students across Cambridge and the surrounding areas, including families in Newnham, Trumpington, Chesterton, and Cherry Hinton. Sessions are available online or in person, and we match each student with a tutor whose subject knowledge, teaching style, and experience are the right fit for their needs.

Our support covers a wide range of subjects and year groups, including:

  • Primary school core subjects — English, Maths, and Science
  • Key Stage 3 preparation and early secondary transition
  • GCSE and IGCSE subjects across all major disciplines
  • A-Level tuition, including Further Maths, Sciences, Humanities, and Languages
  • University admissions tests and Oxbridge interview preparation

GCSE and A-Level Support

Cambridge sits firmly in OCR territory. Many local schools and colleges — including Hills Road Sixth Form College and Long Road Sixth Form College — follow OCR specifications at both GCSE and A-Level, and our tutors are thoroughly familiar with these syllabuses. Whether your child is working through OCR A-Level Biology, OCR GCSE History, or OCR Mathematics, their tutor will know the mark schemes, the command words, and the common pitfalls that make the difference between a good grade and an outstanding one.

Students at The Perse School and The Stephen Perse Foundation may follow different pathways, including IGCSE qualifications or alternative A-Level specifications. Leading Tuition tutors are experienced across these variations and will always tailor their approach to the specific qualification your child is sitting.

For sixth formers, A-Level support is often about more than content knowledge. At this level, students need to develop independent thinking, strong exam technique, and the ability to construct well-reasoned arguments under pressure. Our tutors work on all of these skills, not just subject recall.

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

Cambridge does not have grammar schools in the traditional sense, so the high-stakes 11+ preparation that dominates in some other parts of England is less of a feature here. However, that does not mean primary-age and early secondary students have nothing to prepare for. Entry to independent schools such as St Mary's School Cambridge or The Perse School involves its own admissions process, and many families want their children to be academically confident and well-prepared as they move through primary school and into Year 7.

Leading Tuition supports younger students with core subject foundations, reading comprehension, mathematical reasoning, and verbal skills — building the kind of solid academic grounding that serves children well whatever path they take. Early investment in these skills pays dividends at GCSE and beyond.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

Cambridge has an unusually high proportion of students applying to Oxbridge and other highly competitive universities. For many families in the city, this is not an exceptional ambition — it is a realistic one. Leading Tuition takes this seriously and offers structured support for the full admissions journey.

For students applying to Cambridge or Oxford, preparation goes well beyond predicted grades. Personal statement support, subject-specific admissions tests, and interview coaching all play a significant role. Depending on the course, students may need to prepare for tests such as the MAT (Mathematics Admissions Test), the LNAT (Law National Aptitude Test), the TSA (Thinking Skills Assessment), the HAT (History Aptitude Test), or the ELAT (English Literature Admissions Test). Our tutors have direct experience with these assessments and know what examiners are looking for.

For students applying to Medicine, Dentistry, or Veterinary Science, UCAT preparation is a key part of the process. Leading Tuition provides focused UCAT coaching alongside support for medical school personal statements and MMI interview preparation.

We also support students applying to competitive courses at Russell Group universities beyond Oxbridge, helping them present their strongest application with confidence.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Cambridge

My child attends Hills Road Sixth Form College and is struggling with OCR A-Level Chemistry — can you help?

Yes. We have tutors with specific experience of the OCR A-Level Chemistry specification, including the practical endorsement and the style of extended-answer questions that appear in Papers 1 and 2. We can work on whichever units your child finds most challenging and help them develop the exam technique the specification rewards.

We live in Trumpington — do you offer in-person tutoring or is everything online?

We offer both. Many of our Cambridge families choose online sessions for the flexibility they provide, but in-person tuition is available depending on the tutor and location. When you get in touch, we will discuss what works best for your family and find the right arrangement.

My daughter is applying to Cambridge University to read Natural Sciences — what admissions support do you offer?

We provide comprehensive support for Natural Sciences applicants, including help with the personal statement, preparation for any subject-specific written work requested at shortlisting, and structured interview coaching. Cambridge interviews for NatSci are academically rigorous and problem-based, and our tutors prepare students to think clearly and communicate their reasoning under that kind of pressure.

My son is in Year 5 at a Cambridge primary school — is it too early to start tuition?

Not at all, provided the approach is right. At this stage, the goal is to build genuine confidence and strong foundations in English and Maths, not to drill exam technique. A good tutor will make sessions engaging and age-appropriate while ensuring your son is developing the skills that will support him through secondary school and beyond.
