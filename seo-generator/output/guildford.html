Expert tutors supporting families across Guildford

Guildford sits at the heart of one of the most educationally competitive corners of England. Families here are navigating intense 11+ preparation for the Royal Grammar School Guildford, while parents of older students are increasingly focused on A-Level results strong enough to open doors to London's top universities and Oxbridge. Whether your child is seven years old and just beginning to think about selective entry, or seventeen and preparing for university applications, the academic expectations in this part of Surrey are high — and the support available needs to match them.

Tutoring in Guildford — What We Offer

Leading Tuition works with families across Guildford and the surrounding area, including families in Merrow, Shalford, and further afield in Godalming and Woking. We offer one-to-one tuition delivered online, which means your child receives focused, personalised attention from a specialist tutor without the disruption of travel — particularly useful for busy families managing school runs, clubs, and everything else that fills a week.

Our tutors are subject specialists, not generalists. We match each student carefully based on their school, their exam board, their year group, and their specific gaps. That matching process matters: a student at Guildford High School preparing for GCSE Chemistry on OCR has different needs from a student at George Abbot School working through AQA English Literature. We take those differences seriously from the very first session.

GCSE and A-Level Support

The majority of secondary schools in and around Guildford — including George Abbot School and St Catherine's School — follow AQA and OCR specifications across core subjects. Our tutors are thoroughly familiar with both exam boards and teach directly to the specification your child is being assessed on, rather than offering a generic version of the subject.

At GCSE, we support students across all the core subjects: Mathematics, English Language and Literature, the Sciences, History, Geography, and Modern Foreign Languages. At A-Level, demand is particularly strong in Mathematics, Further Mathematics, Biology, Chemistry, Physics, and Economics — subjects where the jump in difficulty from GCSE is steep and where a strong tutor can make a significant difference to both confidence and results.

For students at Tormead School or Guildford High School working towards A-Levels with Oxbridge or Russell Group applications in mind, we also help with the broader academic skills that competitive universities look for: extended writing, independent thinking, and the ability to engage with material beyond the syllabus.

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

The 11+ in Surrey is genuinely demanding, and the competition for places at the Royal Grammar School Guildford is fierce. RGS is one of the highest-performing grammar schools in the country, and the boys who earn a place there have typically prepared thoroughly — not just in the months before the exam, but over a sustained period.

The RGS entrance process assesses verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, mathematics, and English comprehension. Many families begin structured preparation in Year 4 or early Year 5, and the most effective preparation is consistent and cumulative rather than a last-minute push. Our tutors understand the specific demands of this assessment and work with each child at a pace that builds genuine understanding rather than surface-level familiarity with question types.

We focus on:

  • Verbal and non-verbal reasoning skills developed progressively over time
  • Mathematics fluency at and beyond the expected curriculum level
  • English comprehension and written expression
  • Exam technique, timing, and managing pressure on the day
  • Building the child's confidence alongside their academic ability

We also work with families preparing for independent school entry at 11+ and 13+, including assessments for schools such as Tormead School and Guildford High School, where entrance papers have their own particular character and expectations.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

Each year, Leading Tuition supports a number of students from Surrey applying to study Medicine, Dentistry, and other highly competitive courses. For medical applicants, this means preparation for the UCAT — now used by Oxford, Cambridge, Imperial, and the majority of UK medical schools — as well as interview coaching and personal statement support.

UCAT preparation is most effective when it begins early in Year 12, allowing students to build the abstract reasoning and decision-making skills the test demands without it colliding with the pressure of A-Level examinations. Our tutors guide students through each section of the UCAT systematically, with timed practice and detailed feedback throughout.

Beyond Medicine, we support students applying to Law, Economics, Engineering, and other competitive degree programmes — helping them articulate their academic interests clearly and prepare for any admissions assessments or interviews their chosen universities require.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Guildford

When should we start preparing for the Royal Grammar School Guildford 11+ exam?

Most families who are serious about RGS begin structured preparation in Year 4 or the first term of Year 5. The assessment is competitive enough that a few weeks of cramming rarely produces the results families hope for. Starting earlier allows your child to build skills gradually and approach the exam with genuine confidence rather than last-minute pressure.

My daughter is at Guildford High School and struggling with OCR A-Level Chemistry — can you help?

Yes. We have tutors who specialise in OCR A-Level Chemistry and are familiar with the specific demands of that specification, including the practical endorsement and the style of questions in the written papers. We would match her with a tutor who knows the course well and can identify exactly where the gaps are.

Do you offer tuition for students in Godalming or do you only cover central Guildford?

All of our tuition is delivered online, so location within the area makes no difference. We work with families across Guildford, Godalming, Merrow, Shalford, Woking, and beyond. Your child receives the same quality of tuition regardless of where you live.

How do you decide which tutor to match my child with?

We take into account your child's school, year group, exam board, specific subject, and the nature of the difficulty they are experiencing — whether that is a conceptual gap, exam technique, confidence, or something else. We then select from our team of specialists accordingly. If for any reason the match does not feel right after the first session, we will find a better fit.
