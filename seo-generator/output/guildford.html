Guildford sits at the heart of one of the most educationally competitive corners of England. Families here face real pressure: securing a place at Royal Grammar School Guildford through the 11+ is an ambition shared by hundreds of children each year, and at the other end of the school journey, parents in Surrey are increasingly focused on A-Level results that open doors to London's top universities and to Oxford and Cambridge. Whether you are just beginning to think about entrance exams or supporting a sixth-former through a demanding set of A-Level subjects, Leading Tuition works with Guildford families at every stage of that journey.

Tutoring in Guildford — What We Offer

Leading Tuition provides one-to-one private tuition for pupils from primary age through to sixth form and beyond. Our tutors are subject specialists, not generalists, and every tutor is matched carefully to your child's specific needs, learning style, and school curriculum. Sessions are available online or in person, giving families across Guildford and the surrounding areas — including Merrow, Shalford, and Godalming — the flexibility to fit tuition around busy school and family schedules.

We work with pupils attending a wide range of local schools, including Guildford High School, Tormead School, St Catherine's School, and George Abbot School, as well as those at state primaries preparing for selective entry. Because we understand the specific syllabuses, teaching styles, and expectations of these schools, our support is always relevant and targeted rather than off-the-shelf.

GCSE and A-Level Support

The GCSE and A-Level years are where the stakes feel highest for many families. A dip in confidence in one subject can have a knock-on effect across a pupil's entire timetable, and the pressure to perform consistently is considerable. Our tutors work across all major subjects at both levels, with particular strength in Mathematics, the Sciences, English, History, and Modern Foreign Languages.

In Guildford, the dominant exam boards are AQA and OCR, and our tutors know these specifications in detail. Whether your child is working through OCR A-Level Chemistry, AQA GCSE English Literature, or OCR Mathematics, their tutor will be familiar with the exact mark scheme expectations, the style of questions that appear in each paper, and the common areas where students lose marks unnecessarily. That specificity makes a genuine difference to results.

For A-Level students with ambitions to study at competitive universities, we also help with subject-specific preparation that goes beyond the syllabus — building the depth of understanding that admissions tutors at leading institutions are looking for.

11+ and Grammar School Preparation

The 11+ examination for entry to Royal Grammar School Guildford is one of the most competitive in the country. Each year, a large number of well-prepared boys sit the exam for a limited number of places, and the standard required is consistently high. Families in areas like Woking and Farnham also look to RGS Guildford as a destination school, which means the competition extends well beyond the town itself.

The RGS 11+ tests verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, mathematics, and English comprehension. Success depends not just on raw ability but on familiarity with the format, confidence under timed conditions, and the ability to apply skills consistently across different question types. Many children who are more than capable of thriving at grammar school level underperform simply because the exam format is unfamiliar.

Our 11+ preparation typically includes:

  • An initial assessment to identify your child's current strengths and the areas that need most attention
  • Structured work on verbal and non-verbal reasoning, with progressive difficulty
  • Mathematics consolidation aligned to the level expected at selective entry
  • Timed practice papers with detailed feedback on where marks are being lost
  • Confidence-building strategies so your child approaches exam day feeling prepared, not overwhelmed

We begin working with most 11+ families in Year 4 or early Year 5, though we can also support families who come to us later in the process. Every preparation plan is built around your child's individual starting point.

Medicine and University Admissions Preparation

For sixth-formers in Guildford with ambitions to study Medicine, Dentistry, or other highly competitive courses, the university admissions process involves considerably more than strong A-Level grades. The UCAT (University Clinical Aptitude Test) is now used by the majority of UK medical schools, and performance in this test plays a significant role in determining whether a candidate receives an interview invitation.

Leading Tuition offers structured UCAT preparation, helping students understand the format of each subtest, develop the time management skills the exam demands, and build their scores through targeted practice. We also support students preparing for Oxbridge admissions tests and subject interviews, working with them to develop the kind of analytical, independent thinking that tutors at Oxford and Cambridge are looking for in a candidate.

Frequently Asked Questions about Tutoring in Guildford

When should we start preparing for the Royal Grammar School Guildford 11+ exam?

Most families begin structured preparation in Year 4 or the autumn term of Year 5, which allows enough time to cover the full range of skills without creating unnecessary pressure. That said, if you are starting later, a focused and well-planned programme in Year 5 can still be highly effective. The key is understanding where your child currently stands and building from there.

My daughter attends Guildford High School and is struggling with OCR A-Level Biology — can you help?

Yes. We have tutors who specialise in OCR A-Level Biology and are familiar with the depth of content and the style of extended-answer questions that this specification requires. We would match your daughter with a tutor who knows the course well and can work through the specific topics and past paper questions that are causing difficulty.

Do your tutors come to us in Godalming, or is everything online?

We offer both. Many families in Godalming and the wider Guildford area choose online sessions for the convenience and consistency they provide, particularly during busy term time. In-person tuition is also available depending on location and tutor availability. We are happy to discuss which format would suit your child best.

Our son is applying to study Medicine and needs UCAT support — do you offer that in Guildford?

We do. Our UCAT preparation is available to students across the Guildford area and is delivered online, which means your son can work with a specialist tutor regardless of his school or sixth-form timetable. We cover all five UCAT subtests and help students develop both the skills and the exam technique needed to perform consistently under timed conditions.
